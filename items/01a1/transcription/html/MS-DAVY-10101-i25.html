Lipari

A commune including six of seven islands of the Aeolian Islands located in the Tyrrhenian Sea off the northern coast of Sicily in southern Italy.

Vulcanello

A small peninsula on Vulcano with black-sand beaches & the Valle dei Mostri’s grotesque lava rock formations.

Vulcano

a small volcanic island belonging to Italy in the Tyrrhenian Sea, about 20 km (12 mi) north of Sicily and located at the southernmost end of the seven Aeolian Islands.

Bay of Naples

The Bay (or Gulf) of Naples is located on the south-western coast of Italy, with Naples to the north, Vesuvius to the east, and the Sorrento Peninsula to the south.

Porphyry

A hard, igneous purplish-red rock in which crystals of feldspar are embedded in a fine-grained groundmass.

✕

Pumice

a type of extrusive volcanic rock, produced when lava with a very high content of water and gases is discharged from a volcano.

Strabo

Greek geographer, philosopher, and historian who lived in Asia Minor during the transitional period of the Roman Republic into the Roman Empire.
