JohnLocke

English philosopher and physician, widely regarded as one of the most influential of early Enlightenment thinkers. FRSL.

✕

DavidHartley

Associationist, whose work argued that the vibrations of nerves created sensation. FRSL.

✕

Martin vanMarum

Dutch physician, natural philosopher, and inventor; Director of the Dutch Society of Sciences. ForMemRSL.

✕

JohnCuthbertson

English-born scientific instrument maker who spent much of his career in the Netherlands. Working with Van Marum, he developed glass-plated frictional electrical machines.

✕

WilhelmHisinger

Swedish physicist and chemist who collaborated with Jöns Jacob Berzelius.

✕

Jöns JacobBerzelius

Swedish chemist. He discovered the chemical elements cerium and selenium, and was the first to isolate silicon, thorium, titanium, and zirconium. A rival of Davy's whom he met a few times.

✕

Christoph HeinrichPfaff

German physician, chemist and physicist who worked on animal electricity alongside Volta and Humboldt. The younger brother of mathematician Johann Friedrich Pfaff.

✕

Voltaic

Refers to the current generated by voltaic pile, invented by Italian chemist Alessandro Volta.
