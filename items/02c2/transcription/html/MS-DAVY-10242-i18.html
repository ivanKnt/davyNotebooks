JosephBanks

A member of the Lincolnshire gentry, Banks sailed as a botanist on James Cook's first voyage to the Pacific (1768-1771). Elected President of the Royal Society of London in 1778 he served for forty-two years until just before his death. The dominant figure in the running of English science during that time, he became one of Davy's patrons and ultimately Davy succeeded him to the Presidency. FRSL.

CharlesBlagden

Physician who kept a revealing diary for most of his life. A friend of Joseph Banks serving as a Secretary of the Royal Society of London, 1784-1797; Knighted 1792. FRSL

✕

Daniel CarlssonSolander

Swedish-born naturalist who worked at the British Museum cataloguing their natural history collections according to the Linnean system. Close to Banks, he accompanied him on various vayages including the Endeavour expedition to the Pacific. FRSL.
