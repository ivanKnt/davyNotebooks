aeriform

Meaning having the form or nature of air; gaseous.

✕

Digester

A device invented by French physicist Denis Papin in 1679. It is an early form of pressure cooker. It was known as a bone digester because of its use to extract the fat from bones under the high pressure of steam.

✕

Ether

In Davy's time this likely referred to the colorless, highly volatile, flammable liquid derived from ethyl alcohol by the action of sulphuric acid. It was used as a solvent and as an inhalant anesthetic. In the present day ether refers to a class of compounds that contain an ether group: an oxygen atom connected to two alkyl or aryl groups.
