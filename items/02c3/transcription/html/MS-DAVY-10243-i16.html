AlessandroVolta

Professor of experimental physics at the University of Pavia. At the end of the eighteenth century he Invented the Voltaic pile which produced electric current and which Davy shortly dubbed the battery. Davy described meeting him in 1814 in notebook 14/I, p.38. ForMemRSL.

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.

✕

Boron

Symbol/formula: B

A non-metallic solid substance, believed to be a metal before it was isolated by Davy.

✕

Oxalic acid

Symbol/formula: (CO2H)2

An organic acid with the systematic name ethanedioic acid.

✕

Benzoic acid

Symbol/formula: C6H5COOH

The simplest benzene-based carboxylic acid, which has been known since the 16th century. One of its discoverers was the legendary clairvoyant Nostradamus. Its most common natural source is gum benzoin, a resin found in the bark of trees of the genus Styrax.
