their capacity for electricity
increases "& the fluid is

this agency of Heat "- The Tourmalin

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

Tourmaline

Tourmaline is a boron silicate. Opaque and black in colour, tourmaline is a semi-precious stone that can be found in a variety of colours based on the presence of iron, magnesium, or lithium. Boron was first isolated by Davy in 1808.
