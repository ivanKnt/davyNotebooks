- - The trees possessing the firmest & least
porous heart wood are the longest in
duration - Amongst our own trees need
I say the oak stands pre-eminent &
the Spanish chesnut comes very near
to it for though less rigid in texture it
is much less vascular - In our old
Gothic buildings there has been some
difficulty in distinguishing the Spanish
Chesnut from the Oak - but they may
be easily known by this circumstance
The pores in the Alburnum of the oak
are much larger and thickly set & are
easily distinguished by the unassisted eye
- whilst the pores in the chesnut are
with difficulty seen. -
