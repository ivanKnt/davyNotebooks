- - Many ridiculous ideas formerly
prevailed amongst gardeners on the
subject of change by engrafting -
It has been supposed that mixed
fruits would be procured in this way
& though Ld Bacon with that strength
of reason & sagacity of observation which
so peculiarly belong to him denied
the possibility of such a circumstance -
yet the idea has been lately revived
- Thus in the first Vo of the Comn to the
B A Mr Jay states "that there is
an apple existing at New York sweet
on one side & sour on the other produced
by grafting two slit scions - one from
a tree yielding sweet fruit - the other
from a tree yielding sour fruit on
the same stock" & I have heard
the warm & bitter qualities of
some cherries steeped in brandy
ascribed to them having been
produced from a graft on a
laurel tree. -
