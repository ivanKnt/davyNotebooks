Thomas AndrewKnight

Knight was a botanist and horticulturalist. He was the second president of the Royal Horticulture Society. Davy frequently fished on Knight's esates in the Welsh Marches. FRSL.

✕

Golden Pippin

A variety of cooking apple with a fruity lemon taste. It is gold with russet dots and has pale yellow flesh. It was first recorded in 1629 and fell out of favour in the 1890s due to its small size.

✕

Redstreak

A variety of apple used in the production of cider, thought to have first appeared in the early 17th century. It was small and pale yellow in colour with numerous red streaks. In 1811 Thomas Knight noted it could no longer be successfully propagated, it is now thought this was due to virus build-up in the tissues of the trees.

✕

Moil

A now extinct apple variety formerly used in the production of cider.
