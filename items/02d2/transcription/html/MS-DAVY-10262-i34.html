Ether

In Davy's time this likely referred to the colorless, highly volatile, flammable liquid derived from ethyl alcohol by the action of sulphuric acid. It was used as a solvent and as an inhalant anesthetic. In the present day ether refers to a class of compounds that contain an ether group: an oxygen atom connected to two alkyl or aryl groups.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.
