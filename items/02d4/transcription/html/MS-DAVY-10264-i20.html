Oxalic acid

Symbol/formula: (CO2H)2

An organic acid with the systematic name ethanedioic acid.

✕

Malic acid

Symbol/formula: C4H6O5

An alpha hydroxy acid found in certain fruits and wines. It is used in foods and cosmetics, and sometimes as medicine.

✕

Citric acid

Symbol/formula: C6H8O7

Citric acid occurs naturally in citrus fruits. In biochemistry, it is an intermediate in the citric acid cycle, which occurs in the metabolism of all aerobic organisms.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.
