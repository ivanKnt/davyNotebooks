Turpentine

Turpentine, which is also called oil of turpentine, is a fluid obtained by the distillation of resin harvested from living trees, mainly pines.

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

Ethylene

Symbol/formula: C2H4

Ether

In Davy's time this likely referred to the colorless, highly volatile, flammable liquid derived from ethyl alcohol by the action of sulphuric acid. It was used as a solvent and as an inhalant anesthetic. In the present day ether refers to a class of compounds that contain an ether group: an oxygen atom connected to two alkyl or aryl groups.

Camphor

Symbol/formula: C10H16O

A chemical made by distilling the wood and bark of the camphor tree. Commonly used in lotions and creams to relieve pain and irritation
