Silzer of Berlin in his theory
of pleasures published about the
middle of the last century
had observed that a piece
of Zinc & a piece of
Silver, applied separately to
the tongue & afterwards brought
in contact occasioned a
slightly acid taste. -
This was referred by Fabroni
& Creve to oxydation of the Zinc
& quoted by them in support
of their doctrine. - This is
however merely the exhibition
of a slight degree of electricity
