JosephBlack

From 1766, Professor of Medcine and Chemistry at the University of Edinburgh. Discovered latent heat and fixed air, later renamed carbon dioxide. A correspondent of James Watt and Thomas Beddoes (who studied under him).

Johann FriedrichMayer

German pastor and agriculturalist.

Torbern OlafBergman

Swedish chemist and mineralogist. At some point Davy acquired a set of letters from Bergman to Franz Schwediaur written in the 1780s which are retained in the Royal Institution (RI MS HD/27/A). ForMemRSL.

✕

DavidMacbride

Irish physician, medical writer and lecturer who contributed to the understanding of scurvy. His Experimental Essays (1764) combined the gas theories of Joseph Black and Sir John Pringle.
