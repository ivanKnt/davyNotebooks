JosephBlack

From 1766, Professor of Medcine and Chemistry at the University of Edinburgh. Discovered latent heat and fixed air, later renamed carbon dioxide. A correspondent of James Watt and Thomas Beddoes (who studied under him).

✕

HenryCavendish

A central figure in English natural philosophy and chemistry, among other research he determined the density of the earth and discovered what would later be named hydrogen. He was one of the people who helped appoint Davy to the Royal Institution. Following his death in 1810 Davy delivered a widely-quoted eulogy on Cavendish and later arranged for some of his apparatus to be transferred to the Royal Institution. FRSL.

✕

StephenHales

English clergyman who made major contributions to a range of scientific fields including botany, pneumatic chemistry and physiology. FRSL.

✕

JanIngenhousz

Dutch-born British physiologist, biologist and chemist. He was one of the discoverers of the process of photosynthesis. FRSL.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Hydrocarbonate

Symbol/formula: H2 + CO

Hydrogen mixed with carbon monoxide.

✕

Red lead

Lead (II,IV) oxide. A bright red or solid orange, used in pigment, the manufacture of batteries, and rust-proof primer paints.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

aeriform

Meaning having the form or nature of air; gaseous.
