- The warmth of our habitations in a
great measure depends upon the
continued [xxxxxxxx] |production| & reflection of
heat making rays. - If Heat
passed from Body to body merely
by simple contact or communication.
The quantity of cold air entering
our rooms by different apertures
would be as great as the quantity
of Hot air conceived out of them,
through the chimneys of fire places-
& little or no elevation of temperature
would be produced. -
Radiant Heat, however passes through
all currents of Air. - Its velocity is
impeded by no perturbations, &
undulations, & it is in a constant
state of action & of motion. -
