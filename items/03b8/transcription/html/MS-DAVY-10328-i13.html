- A very ingenious Philosopher ;
in extending the principle of radiation
has supposed, that the earth is cooled
not only by the emission of its own
heat; - but likewise by frigorific
radiations from different parts of
the Heavens. - This source does
not seem to be at all neccessary,
it is founded upon the opinion of
cold as a positive quality; & it
is liable to the same kind of objections,
as those made to the singularly absurd
Theory of the Dutchmen, who aserted
that Night, did not depend upon
the absence of the Solar Light, but
upon the obfuscating influence of
certain heavenly Bodies that acted in
opposition to the Sun, & emitted
rays producing darkness. -

BenjaminThompson

Born in Massachusetts, he served in the British and Bavarian armies. Knighted by George III in 1787, the Elector of Bavaria created him Reichsgraf von Rumford of the Holy Roman Empire in 1792. His appointment in the late 1790s as Bavarian ambassador to London created a minor diplomatic incident, during which he helped establish and manage the Royal Institution, in the course of which he appointed Davy to lecture there. Later he married Lavoisier's widow and lived unhappily ever after. FRSL.
