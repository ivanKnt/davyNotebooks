eudiometrical

Relating to euidometry; the use of an euidometer: a graduated glass tube, closed at one end, that is used for measuring the change in the volume of gases during a chemical reaction.

✕

JosephPriestley

Unitarian and political radical forced into exile in Pennsylvania. As a natural philospher and chemist who retained belief in phlogiston, he discovered oxygen and nitrous oxide though not under those names. FRSL.

✕

AlessandroVolta

Professor of experimental physics at the University of Pavia. At the end of the eighteenth century he Invented the Voltaic pile which produced electric current and which Davy shortly dubbed the battery. Davy described meeting him in 1814 in notebook 14/I, p.38. ForMemRSL.

Thomas CharlesHope

Professor of Medicine and Chemistry at Edinburgh University. Discovered the element strontium and seems to have played a role in Davy's appointment to the Royal Institution. FRSL.

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.
