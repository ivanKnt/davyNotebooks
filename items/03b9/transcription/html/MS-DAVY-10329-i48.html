✕

aeriform

Meaning having the form or nature of air; gaseous.

✕

Eudiometer

A eudiometer is a laboratory device that measures the change in volume of a gas mixture following a physical or chemical change. An early version was used by Priestley in the discovery of oxygen.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.

✕

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

✕

Nitrous acid

Symbol/formula: HNO2

A weak and monoprotic acid known only in solution, in the gas phase and in the form of nitrite salts.

✕

Théodore Turquet deMayerne

Genevean born physician who worked in France and England, where he was royal physician for which he was knighted.

✕

StephenHales

English clergyman who made major contributions to a range of scientific fields including botany, pneumatic chemistry and physiology. FRSL.

✕

HenryCavendish

A central figure in English natural philosophy and chemistry, among other research he determined the density of the earth and discovered what would later be named hydrogen. He was one of the people who helped appoint Davy to the Royal Institution. Following his death in 1810 Davy delivered a widely-quoted eulogy on Cavendish and later arranged for some of his apparatus to be transferred to the Royal Institution. FRSL.
