Geology.
Introductory Lecture
Though very great and important discoveries
have been made with |in| the Science of the Nature
Earth - yet it is still too imperfect
to admit of any one great - independent
& general system. - - " The constitution
& the order, of the external World
can only be understood - by a separate
examination, & minute study - of the
different |classes of| objects that compose it;
|_ and the knowledge of the harmony
& relation of the different parts,
is essential " for a comprehensive
view of the Mechanism of the
whole. -
- In proportion as the various
subjects of Human enquiry have
been separated from each other &
arranged in distinct departments. -"
so in proportion has accurate &
useful knowledge been more extended. -
"By the application of different minds
to different investigations: t No exertions
of genius & labour have been highly lost.
