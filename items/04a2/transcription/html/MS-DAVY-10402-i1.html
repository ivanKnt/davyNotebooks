- So different are the exertions
of the faculties of the mind, & so
infinitely various the combinations of our
ideas; that the same objects may be
examined with the most opposite
views, & considered under many
diversified and beautiful relations. -
- - It is on this fact of our nature
so familiar to every understanding, that
the great extent and progression of Science
& Philosophy depend. - -
- Hence their division into various branches
& hence the distinctness & accuracy of the
different species of Knowledge
- - The Planet that we inhabit may be
considered in its connection with the general
System of the Universe, as acted upon by
