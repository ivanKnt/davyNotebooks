- In Nature there are two grand
species of relation, between phenomena;
- In one, an infinite variety of effects
is produced by one |a single| cause. - In the
other, - a great variety of causes is
subservient to one effect. - both
are equally sublime |important| & equally
worthy of being studied; |_ though
the last has been least attended to,
as the enquiry is more laborious;
& the results less attainable. -
Instances of it, may however, every
where be found. | as parallel to| It prevails generally,
those in Geology.
and the most important series of events
belonging to the external Universe
arise in all their extent & efficacy,
from many diversified combinations.
- A variety of rays co-operate,
to produce the simple sensation of
pure light. - - -
