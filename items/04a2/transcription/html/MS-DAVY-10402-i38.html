I admire thisat Sagacity & genius,
which have exalted him to the
rank of the first Crystallograper
of the Age; - But I cannot
avoid, censuring his Nomenclature. -
- The Names are long & complex,
& they signify properties of the
substances, which are usually
indeterminate, & seldom or never
Characteristic. -
- - The English |British| Nomenclature, the
one in Common use, is undoubtedly
very imperfect & very vague; but at
least it involves no sources of
theoretical error - |_ The
multiplication of words for the
expression of the same Substances,
is a great & an increasing evil,
in the Science, & it ought to be
