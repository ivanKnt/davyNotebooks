- The Atmosphere in which we breathe
& move, & which every where invelopes
the surface, is an immense body of elastic
matter, extending to at least 40 miles
above the Earth. It is extremely moveable
in its parts, & susceptible of the impression
of heat. - The currents arising from its
motions, constitue Winds. One of its
elements is the most active substance in
nature; & it is equally subservient to
the purposes of vitality, in its direct
operation upon living Beings, & in its
more remote effects on the surface which
they inhabit. -
The Atmosphere is the great Theatre of
Electrical Changes; the grandeur &
magnificence of which are displayed
in the thunder Storm; & which are
efficient in a more tranquil series of
operations, belonging to the daily & constant
Order of Events. -
