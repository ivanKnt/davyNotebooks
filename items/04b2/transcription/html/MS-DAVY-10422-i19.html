- Dry Muriate of Lime & powdered
ice or snow liquify by being
rubbed together
An amalgam of lead
& an amalgam of Bismuth
are in the same case
instance.
And if phosphorus
& oxye of Potash be
gently rubbed together
an explosion with light
& heat loudly appeals
against the truth of
the principle.
Instance
- In general however solid
Bodies can be made to
act on each other
only with great difficulty

Muriate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCl2

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline solid at room temperature. Calcium chloride is highly soluble in water, and can be created by neutralising hydrochloric acid with calcium hydroxide.

Bismuth

Symbol/formula: Bi
