Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

JohnMurray

Scottish physician and chemist. Wrote Elements of Chemistry (1801), Elements of Materia Medica and Pharmacy (1804) and A System of Chemistry (1806–7). Murray argued against Davy's interpretation of chlorine as an element and supported the view that the substance contained oxygen.

✕

JohnDavy

Humphry Davy's brother, born in Penzance. Spent some time working with Davy in the Royal Institution before studying medicine at Edinburgh University. Appointed an Army physician he was elevated to the rank of Inspector General of Army Hospitals in the West Indies. Following his brother's death, Davy's notebooks came into his possession which he used in writing biographical studies that strongly defended his reputation. FRSL.
