Some arguments have been raised in
favour of the existence of a specific
fluid of heat, from the circumstances
of the communication of heat to bodies
in exhausted receivers, & from the
manner in which they are affected
by this heat; but there are no
means known to experimental science
of producing a perfect vacuum; even
the best Torricellian vacuum
must contain elastic matter.
