Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Antimony

Symbol/formula: Sb

A lustrous gray metalloid, it is found in nature mainly as the sulphide mineral stibnite. Antimony compounds have been known since ancient times and were powdered for use as medicine and cosmetics, often known by the Arabic name 'kohl'.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.

✕

Arsenic

Symbol/formula: As

Arsenic occurs in many minerals, usually in combination with sulphur and metals, but also as a pure elemental crystal. Arsenic is a metalloid.
