1. Expt. to ascertain whether Ammonia
can be formed without the contact of air.
About 2 parts of powdered charcoal
& 1 of dried pearlash were mixed
together & heated intensely in an iron
tube. The whole of the apparatus
was air tight. - When a considerable
quantity of gas had escaped - the water
connected at one end was boiled in order
to expel the common air yet remaining
in this tube; When this last was effected
the whole of the gas in the receiver was
drawn out by means of a curved glass tube. -
The process was then continued for about
1/2 hour & while it was the barrel was
cooling some vapour of water was passed
through it It was found after
some time that the apparatus [was] not tight

Hydrofluoric acid. A solution of hydrogen fluoride in water.

Silex

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

Muriatic acid

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

Saline

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Sodium chloride in solution.

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Pearlash

Symbol/formula: K2CO3

A white amorphous or granular substance which consists principally of potassium carbonate and has a strong alkaline reaction.

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).
