Tried a number of expts on Tellurium -
- It revived lead from Zinc & Tellurium
in solutions - It combined with
Potassium producing a most intense [fire]
heat & formed a Metallic alloy which
hissed with water. but gave no Hydrogene
& produced the purple tincture which
became revived Tellurium by exposure to
air. - -
When Tellurium was brought in
contact with potash . there seemed
to be formed the Hydruret of Tellurium
& a compound of Tellurium & Potassium
which of course give no Hydrogene by
the action of Water.-

Tellurium

Symbol/formula: Te

A brittle, mildly toxic, rare, silver-white metalloid. Tellurium is chemically related to selenium and sulphur, all three of which are chalcogens. It is occasionally found in its native form as elemental crystals.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.
