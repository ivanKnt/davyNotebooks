Wolfram

Symbol/formula: W

Also known as tungsten. Tungsten is an exceptionally strong refractory metal. It was identified as a new element in 1781 and first isolated as a metal in 1783.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Alumine

Symbol/formula: AL2O3

Alumine is an archaic name for alumina, which is aluminium oxide.

✕

Silex

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

✕

Glucine

A monoclinic mineral containing beryllium, calcium, hydrogen, oxygen, and phosphorus.

✕

Silicon

Symbol/formula: Si

After an attempt to isolate silicon in 1808, Davy proposed the name 'silicium' for silicon, from the Latin silex, silicis for flint, and adding the '-ium' ending because he believed it to be a metal.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Barytes

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

Now known as barium sulphate (BaSO4), a white crystalline solid, occurring as the mineral barites.

✕

Strontium

Symbol/formula: Sr

An alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.
