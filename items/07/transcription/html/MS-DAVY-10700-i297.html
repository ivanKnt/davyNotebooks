Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Boron

Symbol/formula: B

A non-metallic solid substance, believed to be a metal before it was isolated by Davy.

✕

Strontium

Symbol/formula: Sr

An alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Ox acid

Symbol/formula: Cl

Now known as chlorine. Chlorine is a yellow-green gas at room temperature, with a pungent, irritating odour that is detectable at low concentrations.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).
