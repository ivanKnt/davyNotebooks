✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Iron(II) chloride

Symbol/formula: FeCl2

A chemical compound also known as ferrous chloride. The compound is white, but typical samples are often off-white.

✕

Nitre

Symbol/formula: KNO3

Potassium nitrate. The mineral form is saltpetre, which was useful as a fertiliser and for making gunpowder.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Silex

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.

✕

Potassium

Symbol/formula: K

A silvery white metal that is soft enough to easily cut with a knife. Potassium metal reacts rapidly with atmospheric oxygen to form flaky white potassium peroxide in only seconds of exposure.

✕

Quartz

Symbol/formula: SiO2

A hard, crystalline mineral composed of silica (silicon dioxide).

✕

Fluoric acid

Symbol/formula: HF

Hydrofluoric acid. A solution of hydrogen fluoride in water.
