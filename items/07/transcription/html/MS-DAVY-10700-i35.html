Voltaic

Refers to the current generated by voltaic pile, invented by Italian chemist Alessandro Volta.

✕

Tellurium

Symbol/formula: Te

A brittle, mildly toxic, rare, silver-white metalloid. Tellurium is chemically related to selenium and sulphur, all three of which are chalcogens. It is occasionally found in its native form as elemental crystals.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Oxymuriatic gas

Symbol/formula: Cl

Chlorine gas.

✕

retort

In a chemistry laboratory, a retort is a device used for distillation or dry distillation of substances.

Spirit Lamp

An alcohol burner or spirit lamp is a piece of laboratory equipment used to produce an open flame.
