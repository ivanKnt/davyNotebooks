Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

Nickel

Symbol/formula: Ni

A silvery-white lustrous metal with a slight golden tinge. Nickel is a hard and ductile transition metal.

Colbalt

Symbol/formula: Co

A chemical element, a hard, lustrous, silvery metal.

Tellurium

Symbol/formula: Te

A brittle, mildly toxic, rare, silver-white metalloid. Tellurium is chemically related to selenium and sulphur, all three of which are chalcogens. It is occasionally found in its native form as elemental crystals.

✕

Antimony

Symbol/formula: Sb

A lustrous gray metalloid, it is found in nature mainly as the sulphide mineral stibnite. Antimony compounds have been known since ancient times and were powdered for use as medicine and cosmetics, often known by the Arabic name 'kohl'.

Wolfram

Symbol/formula: W

Also known as tungsten. Tungsten is an exceptionally strong refractory metal. It was identified as a new element in 1781 and first isolated as a metal in 1783.

Molybdenum

Symbol/formula: Mo

Chemical element; discovered in 1778 by Carl Wilhelm Scheele.

✕

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).
