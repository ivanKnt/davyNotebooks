Tellurium

Symbol/formula: Te

A brittle, mildly toxic, rare, silver-white metalloid. Tellurium is chemically related to selenium and sulphur, all three of which are chalcogens. It is occasionally found in its native form as elemental crystals.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Sulphurated hydrogen

Symbol/formula: H2S

Hydrogen sulphide.

✕

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

Spicula

A sharp-pointed crystalline structure.
