Oxide of mercury

Symbol/formula: HgO

An inorganic compound, mercury oxide is red or orange in colour, and a solid at room temperature and pressure. The mineral form montroydite is very rarely found.

✕

Ox acid

Symbol/formula: Cl

Now known as chlorine. Chlorine is a yellow-green gas at room temperature, with a pungent, irritating odour that is detectable at low concentrations.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.
