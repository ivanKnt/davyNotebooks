Copper oxide

Symbol/formula: CuO

Copper II oxide is an inorganic compound formed of the elements copper and oxygen. It can be used in applications such as pottery to produce red, green or blue colourings.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Litharge

One of the natural forms of lead(II) oxide. Litharge originally meant the mineral residue from silver refining.
