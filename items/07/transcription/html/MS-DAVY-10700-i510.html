Allen & Howard

The partnership of William Allen (1770-1843) and Luke Howard (1772-1864), from whom Davy bought chemicals and apparatus.

✕

Acetate of lead

Symbol/formula: Pb(CH3COO)2

Also known as lead acetate, lead diacetate, plumbous acetate, sugar of lead, lead sugar, salt of Saturn, or Goulard's powder. A white crystalline chemical compound with a slightly sweet taste.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Bismuth

Symbol/formula: Bi

A high-density, silvery, pink-tinged metal.

✕

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.

✕

D°

Davy's shorthand for 'ditto' (meaning, the same thing again; a repetition of something; an indication of agreeing with something already said so as to avoid repetition).
