Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Oxymuriatic gas

Symbol/formula: Cl

Chlorine gas.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

CharlesHatchett

He made his money from coach building which he used to pursue his interests in chemistry and mineralogy in the course of which he isolated the chemical element now called niobium. He was a Manager of the Royal Institution, playing a key role especially in running its lecture programme during the 1800s. FRSL.

✕

William ThomasBrande

Originally trained as an apothecary, Brande became a chemist. With Davy and others founded the Animal Chemistry Club. Lectured on chemistry in various London institutions and, following Davy's retirement succeeded him in his positions at the Royal Institution where he founded The Quarterly Journal of Science. A Secretary of the Royal Society of London, 1816-1826; later fell out with Davy. FRSL.
