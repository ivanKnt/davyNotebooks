[Tenth] of August 10. -
Exposed a mixture of equal
parts of Oxymuriatic gas &
Nitrous oxide for two days
during which time the sun shone
bright at intervals. - No change
of colour. - Mercury admitted
little or no absorption. - the
Mercury was soon tarnished as
by common chlorine - this gas
agitated with Water - the chlorine
absorbed - a taper burnt in the
residue as in Nitrous oxide .
The smell not quite that
of common chlorine . -
About 6 cubic inches Nitrous
about 12 chlorine
were exposed to Light. - The
nitrous gas being first admitted
a redness was produced from the
residual common air . -

Oxymuriatic gas

Symbol/formula: Cl

Chlorine gas.

✕

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).
