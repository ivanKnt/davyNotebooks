Olefiant gas & might be separated
from the olefiant by Water - The
olefiant mixed with the gas burnt
with it's usual orange |yellow| flame but

in addition - owing to the new gas -

containing several grains of recently

inch of carbonic acid gas over Lime . -

Ethylene

Symbol/formula: C2H4

Ethylene is a flammable hydrocarbon gas.

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.
