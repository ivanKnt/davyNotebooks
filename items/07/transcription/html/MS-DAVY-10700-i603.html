5 grs Metallic palladm. dissolved in pure Nit. Mure.
precipd. by Lime water when nearly neutral. -
- well washed - of a deep brown colour
- dried for some hours in a Platina Crucible
on Sandbath - then just below redness
weighed 5 1/8 1/16 1/32 grs, & approached a blk colour. -
The loss on the filter probably equalled what
would make it 5 1/2 grs which would give
90 palladm. & 10 oxye . - -
But little confidence can be placed in a
single expt,, of this kind. & where the quantity operated
on was so small. -
10 grs fine gold dissolved in Nit. Murc. - precipd.
when nearly neutral by pure pot. At first
precipe. yellow - but after some hours though kept
in the dark, it became of a dull purple.
The precipe. carefully washed & dried [x] weighed
only 8/16 of a grain. - On evaporating to dryness
more of the gold was obtained. potash would
not produce any further precipe. - Seems to form
a triple salt with oxide of Gold. -

Calcium hydroxide

Symbol/formula: Ca(OH)2

Produced when quicklime is mixed with water. Traditionally called slacked lime.

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.
