Hydrat of Tin dried by the sand bath
to Purify Manganese ore sent by Mr Watts containg
carbonate of Lime ~ iron and Manganese - as follows
to the ore in fine powder added Muriatic acid with
3 times its measure of water diluted untill there was
no farther effervescence then washed & dried the residuum
and exposed it to heat with Muriatic acid in a crucible
to expel the iron

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Carbonate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcium carbonate.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.
