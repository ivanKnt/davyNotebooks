Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Silicon

Symbol/formula: Si

After an attempt to isolate silicon in 1808, Davy proposed the name 'silicium' for silicon, from the Latin silex, silicis for flint, and adding the '-ium' ending because he believed it to be a metal.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Litmus

A water-soluble mixture of different dyes extracted from lichens used to test materials for acidity.

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.
