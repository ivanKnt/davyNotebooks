JohnNewman

Scientific instrument maker of Lisle Street, and afterwards of Regent-street. Instrument maker to the Royal Institution who made the earliest Davy safety lamps.

✕

CharlesHatchett

He made his money from coach building which he used to pursue his interests in chemistry and mineralogy in the course of which he isolated the chemical element now called niobium. He was a Manager of the Royal Institution, playing a key role especially in running its lecture programme during the 1800s. FRSL.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Carbonate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcium carbonate.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Sirium

Also known as vestium. Sirium was a name used by Lorenz Chrysanth von Vest (1776-1840) for a substance he believed to be a new metal. Davy, Michael Faraday, and William Hyde Wollaston later identified it as a sulphuret containing iron, nickel, cobalt, and arsenic.

✕

Bismuth

Symbol/formula: Bi

A high-density, silvery, pink-tinged metal.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Barytes

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

Now known as barium sulphate (BaSO4), a white crystalline solid, occurring as the mineral barites.

✕

Barium nitrate

Symbol/formula: Ba(NO₃)₂

Inorganic compound that, like most barium salts, is colorless, toxic, and water-soluble. It burns with a green flame and is an oxidizer; the compound is commonly used in pyrotechnics.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.
