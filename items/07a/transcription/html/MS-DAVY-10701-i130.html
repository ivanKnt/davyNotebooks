Benzoic acid

Symbol/formula: C6H5COOH

The simplest benzene-based carboxylic acid, which has been known since the 16th century. One of its discoverers was the legendary clairvoyant Nostradamus. Its most common natural source is gum benzoin, a resin found in the bark of trees of the genus Styrax.
