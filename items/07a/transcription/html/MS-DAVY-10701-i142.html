✕

Steel

Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon with improved strength and fracture resistance compared to other forms of iron.

✕

Nickel

Symbol/formula: Ni

A silvery-white lustrous metal with a slight golden tinge. Nickel is a hard and ductile transition metal.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Antimony

Symbol/formula: Sb

A lustrous gray metalloid, it is found in nature mainly as the sulphide mineral stibnite. Antimony compounds have been known since ancient times and were powdered for use as medicine and cosmetics, often known by the Arabic name 'kohl'.

✕

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

✕

Platinum

Symbol/formula: Pt

A dense, malleable, ductile, highly unreactive, precious, silverish-white transition metal.
