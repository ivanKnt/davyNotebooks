Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Sulphate of potash

Symbol/formula: K2SO4

Potassium sulphate.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.

✕

Silex

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

✕

Iron oxide

Symbol/formula: Fe2O3

Oxide of iron. A dark red solid which occurs as the mineral haematite and as the main component of rust.

✕

Alumine

Symbol/formula: AL2O3

Alumine is an archaic name for alumina, which is aluminium oxide.

ThomasDundas

Scottish-born politician and Whig MP, created (1794) first Baron Dundas of Aske. An early Proprietor of the Royal Institution and an Ordinary Member of the Board of Agriculture, he was a landowner, owning Loftus alum works. FRSL.

✕

Robert CavendishSpencer

Naval officer and courtier. As the son of 2nd Earl Spencer he enjoyed the courtesy title of Lord.

✕

William ThomasBrande

Originally trained as an apothecary, Brande became a chemist. With Davy and others founded the Animal Chemistry Club. Lectured on chemistry in various London institutions and, following Davy's retirement succeeded him in his positions at the Royal Institution where he founded The Quarterly Journal of Science. A Secretary of the Royal Society of London, 1816-1826; later fell out with Davy. FRSL.
