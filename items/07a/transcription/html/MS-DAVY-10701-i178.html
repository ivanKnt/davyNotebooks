Steel

Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon with improved strength and fracture resistance compared to other forms of iron.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

A mineral consisting of barium sulphate.

✕

Iron oxide

Symbol/formula: Fe2O3

Oxide of iron. A dark red solid which occurs as the mineral haematite and as the main component of rust.

✕

Gelatine

A translucent, colourless, flavorless food ingredient, commonly derived from collagen taken from animal body parts.
