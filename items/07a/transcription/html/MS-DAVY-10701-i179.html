John FredericDaniell

First Professor of Chemistry at King's College London. Developed the first battery that produced a constant flow of electricity.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Hepatic gas

Symbol/formula: H2S

Sulphuretted hydrogen.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Sulphate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaSO4

Calcium sulphate, gypsum.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Muriate of silver

Symbol/formula: AgCl

Silver chloride. A white crystalline solid well-known for its low solubility in water and its sensitivity to light.

✕

Steel

Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon with improved strength and fracture resistance compared to other forms of iron.
