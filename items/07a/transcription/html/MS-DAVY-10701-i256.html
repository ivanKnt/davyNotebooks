Muriate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCl2

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline solid at room temperature. Calcium chloride is highly soluble in water, and can be created by neutralising hydrochloric acid with calcium hydroxide.

✕

Sulphate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaSO4

Calcium sulphate, gypsum.

✕

Carbonate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcium carbonate.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Iron oxide

Symbol/formula: Fe2O3

Oxide of iron. A dark red solid which occurs as the mineral haematite and as the main component of rust.

✕

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Boron

Symbol/formula: B

A non-metallic solid substance, believed to be a metal before it was isolated by Davy.

✕

Barium

Symbol/formula: Ba

Davy was the first to isolate barium, using electrochemistry, in 1808. It is a soft, silvery alkaline earth metal, which due to its high chemical reactivity is never found in nature as a free element.

✕

Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

A mineral consisting of barium sulphate.

✕

Barium nitrate

Symbol/formula: Ba(NO₃)₂

Inorganic compound that, like most barium salts, is colorless, toxic, and water-soluble. It burns with a green flame and is an oxidizer; the compound is commonly used in pyrotechnics.

✕

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Barytes

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

Now known as barium sulphate (BaSO4), a white crystalline solid, occurring as the mineral barites.

✕

Strontium

Symbol/formula: Sr

An alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Selenium

Symbol/formula: Se

Chemical element; discovered in 1817 by Jöns Jacob Berzelius.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

HenryCavendish

A central figure in English natural philosophy and chemistry, among other research he determined the density of the earth and discovered what would later be named hydrogen. He was one of the people who helped appoint Davy to the Royal Institution. Following his death in 1810 Davy delivered a widely-quoted eulogy on Cavendish and later arranged for some of his apparatus to be transferred to the Royal Institution. FRSL.
