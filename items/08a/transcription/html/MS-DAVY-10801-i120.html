Marl

Or marle. A loose, unconsolidated earthy deposit, consisting of clay mixed with calcium carbonate.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Silex

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

D°

Davy's shorthand for 'ditto' (meaning, the same thing again; a repetition of something; an indication of agreeing with something already said so as to avoid repetition).

✕

Sammarang

The Samarang was a sixth-rate frigate and twenty-eight-gun Royal Navy ship. He Davy describes the beginnings of his experiments into his system of electrochemical protection for ships’ copper-sheathed hulls.
