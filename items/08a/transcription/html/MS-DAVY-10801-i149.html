Substance in chlorine in retort - little action in common light

rine absorbed - none permittd to orter ultimately chlorine in excess
a solid crystalline matter something like perchloride Carbon and
a fluid [x] here & there moisting the crystals - probabl the Mur Acid
& water of chlorine - opened retort - much Mur acid formed -
blew it out - substance had an odour something like per chloride of
Carbon but more like artificial Camphor

pungen odour (vapour) - not soluble in water - soluble in alcohol

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Camphor

Symbol/formula: C10H16O

A chemical made by distilling the wood and bark of the camphor tree. Commonly used in lotions and creams to relieve pain and irritation
