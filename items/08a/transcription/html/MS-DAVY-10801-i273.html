✕

Litmus

A water-soluble mixture of different dyes extracted from lichens used to test materials for acidity.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Saline

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Sodium chloride in solution.

✕

Muriate of soda

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Also known as sodium chloride, or common salt.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Oil of vitriol

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

Sulphuric acid. A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen.
