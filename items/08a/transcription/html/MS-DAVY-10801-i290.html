Novr. 17. Bone oil. The 3rd & 5th products have been washed
several times & the washings evaporated . They have much less if any
thing than the washings of 1st product & the matter does not seem
interesting :It is tarry oily &c . but partially resoluble in water
and becomes dark coloured in the air like the former product
The washed No. 5 product not ammoniacal at first became so
by being heated in a tube to redness - there is some compound of
nitrogen present .-. The washed No. 5 product heated with Sulphur
was immediately decomposed Carbon was deposited and Ammonia
with excess of sulphuretted hydrogen evolved

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Sulphurated hydrogen

Symbol/formula: H2S

Hydrogen sulphide.

✕

D°

Davy's shorthand for 'ditto' (meaning, the same thing again; a repetition of something; an indication of agreeing with something already said so as to avoid repetition).
