Andes

Mountain range in western South America.

✕

Hydrosulphuret

When sulphuretted hydrogen combines with an equal volume of ammonia and unites to alkalies and oxides, these compounds are called hydrosulphurets.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Acetate of lead

Symbol/formula: Pb(CH3COO)2

Also known as lead acetate, lead diacetate, plumbous acetate, sugar of lead, lead sugar, salt of Saturn, or Goulard's powder. A white crystalline chemical compound with a slightly sweet taste.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Saline

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Sodium chloride in solution.

✕

Muriate of soda

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Also known as sodium chloride, or common salt.

✕

Calcium hydroxide

Symbol/formula: Ca(OH)2

Produced when quicklime is mixed with water. Traditionally called slacked lime.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Arsenic

Symbol/formula: As

Arsenic occurs in many minerals, usually in combination with sulphur and metals, but also as a pure elemental crystal. Arsenic is a metalloid.

✕

Colbalt

Symbol/formula: Co

A chemical element, a hard, lustrous, silvery metal.

✕

Nickel

Symbol/formula: Ni

A silvery-white lustrous metal with a slight golden tinge. Nickel is a hard and ductile transition metal.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.
