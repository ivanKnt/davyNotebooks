✕

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).

✕

Litmus

A water-soluble mixture of different dyes extracted from lichens used to test materials for acidity.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Red lead

Lead (II,IV) oxide. A bright red or solid orange, used in pigment, the manufacture of batteries, and rust-proof primer paints.

✕

Litharge

One of the natural forms of lead(II) oxide. Litharge originally meant the mineral residue from silver refining.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.
