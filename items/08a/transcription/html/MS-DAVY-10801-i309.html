1829. Sept 20.th
Water from beaver and otter houses of Zoological garden
Otters & fish not live in the latter
By examination I can find nothing to account for the unwholesomeness
of the water except - natural circumstances as that otter not used to still
water &c Both contain but little Sul or other acid - the beaver water most
Lime & neither of them any perceptible metallic or poisonous [ingredient].
The otter water is purest as to earthy salts - the [bever] water contains more
carb acid.

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Manganese oxide

Symbol/formula: MnO

Any of a variety of manganese oxides and hydroxides.
