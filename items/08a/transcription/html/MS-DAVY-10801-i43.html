Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Platinum

Symbol/formula: Pt

A dense, malleable, ductile, highly unreactive, precious, silverish-white transition metal.

William ThomasBrande

Originally trained as an apothecary, Brande became a chemist. With Davy and others founded the Animal Chemistry Club. Lectured on chemistry in various London institutions and, following Davy's retirement succeeded him in his positions at the Royal Institution where he founded The Quarterly Journal of Science. A Secretary of the Royal Society of London, 1816-1826; later fell out with Davy. FRSL.
