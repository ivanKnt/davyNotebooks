Uranium

Symbol/formula: U

Discovered in 1789 by Martin Heinrich Klaproth and named after the newly discovered planet, Uranus.

✕

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Fluoboric gas

Symbol/formula: BF3

Boron trifluoride. A corrosive gas produced by the action of hydrofluoric acid on boron oxides.

✕

Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

A mineral consisting of barium sulphate.

✕

Borax

Symbol/formula: Na2[B4O5(OH)4]·8H20

A hydrated or anhydrous borate of sodium. A colourless crystalline solid that dissolves in water to make a basic solution.

✕

Spirit Lamp

An alcohol burner or spirit lamp is a piece of laboratory equipment used to produce an open flame.

✕

D°

Davy's shorthand for 'ditto' (meaning, the same thing again; a repetition of something; an indication of agreeing with something already said so as to avoid repetition).
