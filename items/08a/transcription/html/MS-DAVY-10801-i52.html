Borax

Symbol/formula: Na2[B4O5(OH)4]·8H20

A hydrated or anhydrous borate of sodium. A colourless crystalline solid that dissolves in water to make a basic solution.

✕

Acetate of lead

Symbol/formula: Pb(CH3COO)2

Also known as lead acetate, lead diacetate, plumbous acetate, sugar of lead, lead sugar, salt of Saturn, or Goulard's powder. A white crystalline chemical compound with a slightly sweet taste.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Boric acid

Symbol/formula: B(OH)3

A compound of boron, oxygen, and hydrogen. Davy often refers to this as boracic acid.

✕

Uranium

Symbol/formula: U

Discovered in 1789 by Martin Heinrich Klaproth and named after the newly discovered planet, Uranus.

✕

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

✕

Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

A mineral consisting of barium sulphate.

✕

Alkalimeter

Invented by François-Antoine-Henri Descroizilles in 1791, this was first used to measure the quantity of chlorine in dyes and it is the first use of volumetric titration in chemistry.
