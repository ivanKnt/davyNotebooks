< Ammonia >
March 21.
Tried for Ammonia by Mur amm & pure Lime - did not
succeed because of water found & difficulty of [rubbing] in these small
vessels.
- March. 29th. Prepared Am gas [about] it by Chlo Silver dry -
put it into a bent tube heated one end & cooled the other. Chloride formed
at low temp gave out Amm which condensed into a liquid at
the other end.
Ammonia

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Sulphurated hydrogen

Symbol/formula: H2S

Hydrogen sulphide.

✕

Euchlorine

An archaic term describing a yellow or greenish-yellow gas, supposed to consist of chlorine dioxide with some free chlorine.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Carbonic oxide

Symbol/formula: CO

Now known as carbon monoxide, a colourless, odourless, poisonous and flammable gas.

✕

Ethylene

Symbol/formula: C2H4

Ethylene is a flammable hydrocarbon gas.

✕

Fluoboric gas

Symbol/formula: BF3

Boron trifluoride. A corrosive gas produced by the action of hydrofluoric acid on boron oxides.

✕

Nitrous acid

Symbol/formula: HNO2

A weak and monoprotic acid known only in solution, in the gas phase and in the form of nitrite salts.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.
