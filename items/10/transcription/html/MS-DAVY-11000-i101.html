Aqueous vapour

Also known as water vapour; the gaseous phase of water which can be produced from the evaporation or boiling of water as a liquid, or from the sublimation of ice. Water vapour is invisible to the eye due to its gaseous form.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Ethylene

Symbol/formula: C2H4

Ethylene is a flammable hydrocarbon gas.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Sulphurated hydrogen

Symbol/formula: H2S

Hydrogen sulphide.

✕

Carbonic oxide

Symbol/formula: CO

Now known as carbon monoxide, a colourless, odourless, poisonous and flammable gas.
