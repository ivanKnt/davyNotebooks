Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Society of Arcueil

The Society of Arcueil was a circle of French scientists who met regularly on summer weekends between 1806 and 1822 at the country houses of Claude Louis Berthollet and Pierre-Simon Laplace at Arcueil, then a village three miles south of Paris.

JohnDavy

Humphry Davy's brother, born in Penzance. Spent some time working with Davy in the Royal Institution before studying medicine at Edinburgh University. Appointed an Army physician he was elevated to the rank of Inspector General of Army Hospitals in the West Indies. Following his brother's death, Davy's notebooks came into his possession which he used in writing biographical studies that strongly defended his reputation. FRSL.
