MichaelFaraday

From 1812 Davy used Faraday as his amanuensis and the following year secured his appointment as laboratory assistant at the Royal Institution. Despite a difficult relationship with Davy, Faraday later became the dominent figure at the Royal Institution and one of the leading scientific personalities of the middle third of the nineteenth century. A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.
