Phosphuretted hydrogene

Symbol/formula: PH3

Perhaps because of its strong association with elemental phosphorus, phosphine was once regarded as a gaseous form of the element, but Antoine Lavoisier (1789) recognised it as a combination of phosphorus with hydrogen.

✕

Phosphorus

Symbol/formula: P

A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Phosphoric acid

Symbol/formula: H3PO4

A corrosive acid that can form three different classes of salts, namely primary phosphates, dibasic phosphates, and tribasic phosphates. It is corrosive to ferrous metals and alloys.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.
