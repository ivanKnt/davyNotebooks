Phosphorus

Symbol/formula: P

A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Phosphorus acid

Symbol/formula: H3PO3

Different to phosphoric acid (H3PO4), phosphorus acid is an intermediate in the preparation of other phosphorus compounds.

✕

Phosphoric acid

Symbol/formula: H3PO4

A corrosive acid that can form three different classes of salts, namely primary phosphates, dibasic phosphates, and tribasic phosphates. It is corrosive to ferrous metals and alloys.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Hydrophosphorous acid

In his Royal Society paper 'On Some Combinations of Phosphorus and Sulphur, and on Some Other Subjects of Chemical Inquiry' (Vol 102, 1812), Davy describes his experiments with the fluid compound of phosphorous and oxymuriatic acid, or chlorine. Treated with water, crystals form which Davy calls hydrophosphorous acid, which apparently consists of 'pure phosphorous acid and water' (p. 408).
