Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Elastic fluid

A descriptive term for a gas, emphasising its elastic quality of spontaneous expansion.

✕

ThomasYoung

Linguist, physician, mathematician and natural philosopher amongst other activities. Professor of Natural Philosophy at the Royal Institution, 1801-1803, but was not a good lecturer. Later worked on deciphering the Rosetta stone. Served as Secretary to the Board of Longitude and Foreign Secretary of the Royal Society of London. FRSL.
