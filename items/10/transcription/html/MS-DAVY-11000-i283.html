Friedrich Charles LouisSickler

German archaeologist, antiquarian, author and occasional topographrical draughtsman. 1805-10 taught in Rome; from 1812 director of the Gymnasium in Hildburghausen, and author of many texts used in teaching.

✕

ThomasTyrwhitt

An English politician and MP, Tyrwhitt was responsible for the construction of several roads across Dartmoor, a hamlet called Princetown named in honour of the Prince of Wales, a prison for prisoners of war captured during the Napoleonic Wars now known as HM Prison Dartmoor, as well as the Plymouth and Dartmoor Railway.

✕

ThomasYoung

Linguist, physician, mathematician and natural philosopher amongst other activities. Professor of Natural Philosophy at the Royal Institution, 1801-1803, but was not a good lecturer. Later worked on deciphering the Rosetta stone. Served as Secretary to the Board of Longitude and Foreign Secretary of the Royal Society of London. FRSL.

Herculaneum

Herculaneum was an ancient Roman town, located in the modern-day comune of Ercolano, Campania, Italy.
