Cyanogen

Symbol/formula: (CN)2

A highly toxic and colourless gas, a carbon nitride, synthesised by Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac in 1815.

✕

Oxide of mercury

Symbol/formula: HgO

An inorganic compound, mercury oxide is red or orange in colour, and a solid at room temperature and pressure. The mineral form montroydite is very rarely found.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Joseph LouisGay-Lussac

French chemist and physicist, known mostly for his discovery that water is made of two parts hydrogen and one part oxygen by volume (with Alexander von Humboldt), for two laws related to gases, and for his work on alcohol-water mixtures. Became involved in controversies with Davy over various chemical discoveries. ForMemRSL.
