may be called the fire valve lanterns.
In this the candle or lamp burns
with its full quantity of air, till
the air begins to be mixed

fire damp increases the flame
a thermometrical spring at the top
of the lantern made of Brass & Steel soldered
together & in a curved form |expands| moves
a valve which may be either at
the air hole or the chimney

& extinguishes the flame.

Brass

Brass is an alloy of copper (Cu) and zinc (Zn), in proportions which can be varied to achieve different colours and mechanical, electrical, acoustic, and chemical properties, but copper typically has the larger proportion.

Steel

Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon with improved strength and fracture resistance compared to other forms of iron.

Ethylene

Symbol/formula: C2H4

Ethylene is a flammable hydrocarbon gas.

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.
