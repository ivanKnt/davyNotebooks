7 21
Lighter Ascertained this day March 12
that to a colour of extraordinary brightness said
to be cornine on the walls of Pompeii is vermilion
Ascertained the 10 of March that powdered
antimony , burnt in a mixture of 1 of
chlorine & 2 of common air ; but it did
not burn in Euclorine but afforded
only 1 of Oxygene to 2 of Chl by its
decomposition – Ascertained likewise
that fluoric acid fumes precipitates
a white matter from Chlorinic acid
- which turns out to be a combination or mix
of fluorine lead & iodine
- - fluoric acid made from lead
does not touch iodine at least only
dissolves a very little of it
dissolves oxyiodine; but does not
seem to form a permanent union
& does not destroy it or become
a new body from its action; yet a
little purplish matter precipitated from the
acid is thus a compound of " iodine & fluorine "

Pompeii

An ancient city located in what is now the comune of Pompei near Naples in the Campania region of Italy. Pompeii was buried in volcanic ash in the Eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 CE.

Antimony

Symbol/formula: Sb

A lustrous gray metalloid, it is found in nature mainly as the sulphide mineral stibnite. Antimony compounds have been known since ancient times and were powdered for use as medicine and cosmetics, often known by the Arabic name 'kohl'.

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Fluoric acid

Symbol/formula: HF

Hydrofluoric acid. A solution of hydrogen fluoride in water.

✕

Fluorine

Symbol/formula: F

Carl Scheele’s investigations into fluorspar showed that it was a compound of calcium and an unidentified acid, capable of dissolving glass, released when fluorspar reacted with sulphuric acid. The unidentified substance was first shown to be an element in 1810 by André-Marie Ampère, and subsequently proven by Davy. The element came to be named fluorine through its commonly-known compound of fluorspar.

✕

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.
