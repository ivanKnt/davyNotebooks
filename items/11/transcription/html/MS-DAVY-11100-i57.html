On the methods that may be adopted
for preventing the accidents arising from
explosions of the fire damp in coal
mines. . by Sir. H D.
The accidents arising from the explosion
of the fire damp or inflammable gas of
coal mines mixed with atmospherical air
are actually becoming more frequent
& more terrible in the collieries in the
North of England .-
A committee has been for some time
formed at Sunderland for the |benevolent| purpose of
investigating the causes of these accidents
& of searching for means of preventing
them. the consequence of an invitation
from the Revd Dr Gray are of the
most active members of this committee
