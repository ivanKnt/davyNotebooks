Baths of Titus

Public baths built in Rome in 81 CE by the emperor Titus.

Lixivia

Water containing the alkaline salts leached from wood ashes, and more widely, any liquid that is obtained by the process of leaching.

✕

Borax

Symbol/formula: Na2[B4O5(OH)4]·8H20

A hydrated or anhydrous borate of sodium. A colourless crystalline solid that dissolves in water to make a basic solution.

✕

Nitre

Symbol/formula: KNO3

Potassium nitrate. The mineral form is saltpetre, which was useful as a fertiliser and for making gunpowder.
