✕

Agate

A type of quartz primarily formed within metamorphic and volcanic rocks.

✕

Rosso antico

Type of marble known for its deep, rich red, purple and brown hues. Davy used this to colour glass.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Carmine

A deep crimson pigment obtained from cochineal, the dye made from the dried bodies Coccus cacti insect.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

✕

Lake

A purplish-red pigment made by the mixing of colouring matter made from animal (e.g. insect) or vegetable with some metallic oxide or earth.
