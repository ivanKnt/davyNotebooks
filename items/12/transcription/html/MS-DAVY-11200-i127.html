Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).

✕

Silex

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

✕

Cobalt

Symbol/formula: Co

A chemical element, a hard, lustrous, silvery metal.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Fluate of potassa

A fluate is a salt formed by the fluoric acid combined with a base; fluate of potassa is the combination of fluoric acid and potash.

✕

Fluate

A now obsolete term for a fluoride.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Arsenic acid

Symbol/formula: H3AsO4

Chemical compound with the formula H₃AsO₄, an arsenic analogue of phosphoric acid

✕

Oxyionic acid

Davy is possibly using the term ‘oxyionic acid’ to mean what is later called ‘oxyiodic’ or ‘oxiodic’ acid. He renames the element ‘ione’ as ‘iodine’ in English; however, the ‘n’ rather than the ‘d’ is retained for iodine compounds such as hydroionic acid and chlorionic acid; oxyionic acid retains this consistency. Other writers, however, refer to hydroiodic, chloriodic and oxyiodic acids.

✕

Barium

Symbol/formula: Ba

Davy was the first to isolate barium, using electrochemistry, in 1808. It is a soft, silvery alkaline earth metal, which due to its high chemical reactivity is never found in nature as a free element.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

frit

A word to describe the materials, either partly or fully fused by heating, from which glass is made.
