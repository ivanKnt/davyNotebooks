Voltaic

Refers to the current generated by voltaic pile, invented by Italian chemist Alessandro Volta.

✕

Oxymuriatic acid

Symbol/formula: Cl

Chlorine. Chlorine is a yellow-green gas at room temperature, with a pungent, irritating odour that is detectable at low concentrations.

✕

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).

✕

Nitre

Symbol/formula: KNO3

Potassium nitrate. The mineral form is saltpetre, which was useful as a fertiliser and for making gunpowder.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Cobalt

Symbol/formula: Co

A chemical element, a hard, lustrous, silvery metal.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

William HydeWollaston

Originally a physician he became a prominent chemist, physicist and physiologist isolating the chemical elements osmium, palladium and rhodium. He made his fortune by developing a method of obtaining metallic platinum. A friend and to some extent a rival of Davy's, in 1820 he served briefly as President of the Royal Society of London between Banks's death and Davy's election. FRSL.

Titus CaesarVespasianus

Roman emperor from 79 to 81.

✕

frit

A word to describe the materials, either partly or fully fused by heating, from which glass is made.

✕

Pozzuoli

A city and region in the Italian region of Campania. The source for Pozzuoli Red, a natural earth pigment that was originally found in the volcanic areas of the region.
