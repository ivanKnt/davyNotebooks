Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

Lixivia

Water containing the alkaline salts leached from wood ashes, and more widely, any liquid that is obtained by the process of leaching.

✕

Fucus

Genus of algae or seaweeds (also known as wracks) found in the northern hemisphere. Bladderwrack is one variety; this is the seaweed from which iodine was originally made.

✕

Ulva

The genus ulva refers to sea lettuces, edible green algae found worldwide.
