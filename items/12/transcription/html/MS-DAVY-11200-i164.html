A new and a very curious substance has recently occupied the
attention of chemists at Paris
This substance was accidentally discovered about two years ago by
M. Courtois a manufacturer of saltpetre at Paris. In his processes
for procuring soda from the ashes of sea-weeds (cendres de vareck) he
found the iron vessels employed much corroded and in searching
for the cause of this effect he made the discovery _ The substance is
procured from the ashes after the extraction of the carbonate of Soda
with great facility and merely by the action of the sulphuric
acid_ When the acid is concentrated so as to produce much
heat the substance appears as a vapour of a beautiful violet colour

Carbonate of lime

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.
