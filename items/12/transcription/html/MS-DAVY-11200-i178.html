phenomena were obserd when the chlorionic acid was digested with
white oxide of lead procured by precipitation from nitrate of
lead there seemed to be no separation of chlorine &
iodine & no proof that Iodic acid exists in
this compound. . for the fluid to the last had the
power of destroying vegetable colours & the peculiar
smell of chlorionic acid ;-
With oxide of silver there was a different result
a white precipitate was formed probably muriate
of silver & an acid fluid; which gave
white chrystals by evaporation which retained
acid properties; but when Ammonia was
added to those chrystals , a solid mater
precipitated (not [xx] which blackened like the iode
of silver: & on decomposing the ammoniacal
substance by heat . iodine rose but [xxxx]
remained a substance like the yellow oxide
of silver; so that in this instance a
triple compound only was formed.

Chlorionic acid

In his paper 'Some Experiments on a Solid Compound of Iodine and Oxygene, and on Its Chemical Agencies', published for the Royal Society in 1815, Davy described chlorionic acid as 'the substance produced by the agency of
the combination of iodine and chlorine in water' (Vol 105, 1815).

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

Silver oxide

Symbol/formula: Ag2O

A solid compound soluble in alkali solutions and slightly so in water.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

Euchlorine

An archaic term describing a yellow or greenish-yellow gas, supposed to consist of chlorine dioxide with some free chlorine.
