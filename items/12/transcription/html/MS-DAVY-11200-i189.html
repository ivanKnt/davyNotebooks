. This day. March 6th |1814.| At Genoa
I made a series of expts on Iodine .
& others on the Torpedo & on the decomposition
of the fluates.
- I found that when Muriate of Baryta was
mixed with Potassame there was a slight preicpitate
but on evaporating there seemed to be no
muriate of Pot: formed & both substances fused together
& were decomposed by Sulphuric acid giving iodine
& Muriatic acid . When Muriate of Magnesia
& Muriate of Lime were mixed with Potassame
there were slight precipitates & it seems that
a decomposition had taken place for on
distilling to dryness Iodine rose in vapour.
in distilling the mixture from M.lime in
a close vessel there was fusion, with much
effervescence & Iodine rose, quere was
not water present & Hydrogene produced.
- When Potassame was heated with Boracic
acid it gave a yellow glass which was
partly soluble in water & had no
alkaline properties & from this glass Iodine
& boracic acid were produced by Sulphuric
acid._
When a little Muriatic or sulphuric
acid were added to Potassame its solution
still reddened vegetable blues. turmeric;

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Potassame

Symbol/formula: KIO3

Now known as potassium iodate. A compound of potassium and iodine.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Muriate of magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgCl2

Magnesium chloride. Magnesium chloride salts are colourless or white solids that are highly soluble in water.

✕

Muriate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCl2

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline solid at room temperature. Calcium chloride is highly soluble in water, and can be created by neutralising hydrochloric acid with calcium hydroxide.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Boron

Symbol/formula: B

A non-metallic solid substance, believed to be a metal before it was isolated by Davy.

✕

Boric acid

Symbol/formula: B(OH)3

A compound of boron, oxygen, and hydrogen. Davy often refers to this as boracic acid.

✕

torpedo

An electric ray fish.

quere
