Litmus

A water-soluble mixture of different dyes extracted from lichens used to test materials for acidity.

✕

Silex

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

✕

Potassame

Symbol/formula: KIO3

Now known as potassium iodate. A compound of potassium and iodine.

✕

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Sulfurous acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

Sulphuric acid. A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen.

✕

Iron(II) chloride

Symbol/formula: FeCl2

A chemical compound also known as ferrous chloride. The compound is white, but typical samples are often off-white.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Yttria

Symbol/formula: Y2O3

Yttrium oxide, also known as yttria, is an air-stable, white solid substance.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Glucine

A monoclinic mineral containing beryllium, calcium, hydrogen, oxygen, and phosphorus.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Fluate

A now obsolete term for a fluoride.

✕

Iron(III) chloride

Symbol/formula: FeCl3

A chemical compound also known as ferric chloride. Ferric chloride compounds are some of the most important and commonplace compounds of iron.
