Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Potassame

Symbol/formula: KIO3

Now known as potassium iodate. A compound of potassium and iodine.

✕

Oxypotassame

Davy explains in his paper 'Further Experiments and Observations on Iodine', published in 1814 (Vol 104, see pp. 494-5), that oxyiodes are triple compounds which produce oxygen and iodine when 'decomposed by heat'. Oxypotassame is therefore 'the triple compound of potassium, oxygene, and iodine.'

✕

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Oxyionic acid

Davy is possibly using the term ‘oxyionic acid’ to mean what is later called ‘oxyiodic’ or ‘oxiodic’ acid. He renames the element ‘ione’ as ‘iodine’ in English; however, the ‘n’ rather than the ‘d’ is retained for iodine compounds such as hydroionic acid and chlorionic acid; oxyionic acid retains this consistency. Other writers, however, refer to hydroiodic, chloriodic and oxyiodic acids.

✕

Sulfurous acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

Sulphuric acid. A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen.

✕

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Sulphate of potash

Symbol/formula: K2SO4

Potassium sulphate.

✕

Oxalic acid

Symbol/formula: (CO2H)2

An organic acid with the systematic name ethanedioic acid.

✕

Saline

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Sodium chloride in solution.

✕

Hydro-phosphoric acid

Symbol/formula: PH3

A chemical compound now more commonly known as phosphine or phosphane.

✕

Carbonate of potash

Symbol/formula: K2CO3

Potassium carbonate. It is a white salt, which is soluble in water and forms a strongly alkaline solution.
