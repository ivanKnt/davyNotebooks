Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Carbonate of potash

Symbol/formula: K2CO3

Potassium carbonate. It is a white salt, which is soluble in water and forms a strongly alkaline solution.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Hydroionic acid

Symbol/formula: HI

Now known as hydroiodic acid. This acid is formed from the combination of iodine with hydrogen. Davy discusses making hydrionic acid in his 'Philosophical Transactions' paper 'Some Experiments and Observations on a New Substance Which Becomes a Violet Coloured Gas by Heat', published in 1814.

✕

Glucine

A monoclinic mineral containing beryllium, calcium, hydrogen, oxygen, and phosphorus.

✕

Muriate of Glucine

Symbol/formula: BeCl2

Beryllium chloride. Because of its sweet flavour, the element beryllium was once known as glucine (Gl).

✕

Litmus

A water-soluble mixture of different dyes extracted from lichens used to test materials for acidity.

✕

Yttria

Symbol/formula: Y2O3

Yttrium oxide, also known as yttria, is an air-stable, white solid substance.

✕

Zircon

Symbol/formula: ZrSiO4

A mineral belonging to the group of nesosilicates and a source of the metal zirconium. Its chemical name is zirconium(IV) silicate.

✕

Calcium

Symbol/formula: Ca

Davy was the first to isolate calcium, using electrochemistry, in 1808. It is a reactive metal that forms a dark oxide-nitride layer when exposed to air.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Potassium

Symbol/formula: K

A silvery white metal that is soft enough to easily cut with a knife. Potassium metal reacts rapidly with atmospheric oxygen to form flaky white potassium peroxide in only seconds of exposure.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).
