Calcium hydroxide

Symbol/formula: Ca(OH)2

Produced when quicklime is mixed with water. Traditionally called slacked lime.

✕

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Calcium

Symbol/formula: Ca

Davy was the first to isolate calcium, using electrochemistry, in 1808. It is a reactive metal that forms a dark oxide-nitride layer when exposed to air.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Hydro-phosphoric acid

Symbol/formula: PH3

A chemical compound now more commonly known as phosphine or phosphane.

✕

Phosphorus acid

Symbol/formula: H3PO3

Different to phosphoric acid (H3PO4), phosphorus acid is an intermediate in the preparation of other phosphorus compounds.

✕

Potassium

Symbol/formula: K

A silvery white metal that is soft enough to easily cut with a knife. Potassium metal reacts rapidly with atmospheric oxygen to form flaky white potassium peroxide in only seconds of exposure.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

✕

Sulfurous acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

Sulphuric acid. A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen.

✕

Carbonate of potash

Symbol/formula: K2CO3

Potassium carbonate. It is a white salt, which is soluble in water and forms a strongly alkaline solution.

✕

Potassame

Symbol/formula: KIO3

Now known as potassium iodate. A compound of potassium and iodine.

✕

Phosphoric acid

Symbol/formula: H3PO4

A corrosive acid that can form three different classes of salts, namely primary phosphates, dibasic phosphates, and tribasic phosphates. It is corrosive to ferrous metals and alloys.
