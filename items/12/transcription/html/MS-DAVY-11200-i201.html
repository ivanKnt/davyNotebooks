Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Potassa

Symbol/formula: K2O

Potassium oxide. In his 'Elements of Chemical Philosophy', Pt 1, Vol 1 (1812), Davy describes how potassa is produced from heating potassium at a low temperature, producing a substance containing a large amount of oxygen. When this substance is "heated very strongly", it produces "pure potash or potassa" (pp. 323-324).

✕

Potassame

Symbol/formula: KIO3

Now known as potassium iodate. A compound of potassium and iodine.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Azotane

Symbol/formula: NCI3

French chemists named nitrogen ‘azote’. In his nomenclature of assigning the termination -ane to chlorine compounds, ‘azotane’ is thus Davy’s name for chloride of nitrogen.

✕

Hydroionic acid

Symbol/formula: HI

Now known as hydroiodic acid. This acid is formed from the combination of iodine with hydrogen. Davy discusses making hydrionic acid in his 'Philosophical Transactions' paper 'Some Experiments and Observations on a New Substance Which Becomes a Violet Coloured Gas by Heat', published in 1814.

✕

Muriate of ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH4Cl

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline salt that is highly soluble in water. Solutions of ammonium chloride are mildly acidic. In its naturally occurring mineralogic form, it is known as sal ammoniac.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Potassane

A compound of potassium and chlorine.

✕

Fluate of potassa

A fluate is a salt formed by the fluoric acid combined with a base; fluate of potassa is the combination of fluoric acid and potash.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.
