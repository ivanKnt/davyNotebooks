Jean-BaptisteBiot

French mathematician, physicist and astronomer; Professor of Physics at the Collège de France. ForMemRSL.

✕

Dominique François JeanArago

French mathematician, physicist, astronomer and political liberal. Davy presented him with the Copley Medal in 1825. ForMemRSL.

✕

Joseph LouisGay-Lussac

French chemist and physicist, known mostly for his discovery that water is made of two parts hydrogen and one part oxygen by volume (with Alexander von Humboldt), for two laws related to gases, and for his work on alcohol-water mixtures. Became involved in controversies with Davy over various chemical discoveries. ForMemRSL.

✕

Claude LouisBerthollet

French Chemist. ForMemRSL.

✕

RichardKirwan

Irish chemist, mineralogist and geologist who accused fellow geologist, James Hutton, of atheism following publication of his Theory of the Earth (1788). FRSL

✕

Johann GeorgTralles

German mathematician and physicist

✕

Louis JacquesThénard

Held chairs of chemistry at the Collège de France and the Ecole Polytechnique. Worked closely with Gay-Lussac and met Davy in Paris in 1813. ForMemRSL.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.
