MichaelFaraday

From 1812 Davy used Faraday as his amanuensis and the following year secured his appointment as laboratory assistant at the Royal Institution. Despite a difficult relationship with Davy, Faraday later became the dominant figure at the Royal Institution and one of the leading scientific personalities of the middle third of the nineteenth century. WilliamKerr

Dates untraced; the nephew of Jane Davy's uncle, William Kerr (1738-1824), a physican of Northampton; one of Jane Davy's executors who had been a medical student at the University of Edinburgh and later a physician and assistant surgeon.

✕

JaneDavy

Daughter of Charles Kerr, an Antiguan merchant who made his fortune in highly dubious circumstances. Following his death she moved to Britain where she married Schukbrugh Apreece who died in 1807. After running a successful literary salon in Edinburgh she moved to London where she met Davy and they married on 11 April 1812 two days after he had been knighted by the Prince Regent.

Lesketh How

Built in 1844, and since redeveloped, Lesketh-How was the house near Ambleside, in the Lake District, where Davy's brother, the physician John Davy, lived. John Davy died there in January 1868.

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.
