Diamond

a precious stone consisting of a clear and colourless crystalline form of pure carbon, the hardest naturally occurring substance. Davy was the first to prove that diamond was pure carbon in Italy: 'Some Experiments on the Combustion of the Diamond and Other Carbonaceous Substances', Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London, Vol. 104 (1814), pp. 557-570.

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

Count GirolamoBardi

Italian geologist and mineralogist. He was the director of the Museum of Physics and Natural History of Florence where Davy experimented in 1814.

✕

GiuseppeGazzaris

(Gazzeri) was an Italian chemist who published Compendio d'un trattato elementare di chimica - a chemistry textbook based on the work of the French Chemists.

Domenico LinoMorichini

Italian physican and chemist. Professor of Chemistry at the Univerity of Rome from 1798 to his death. Gave Davy medical advice in his final years. ForMemRSL.
