✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Diamond

a precious stone consisting of a clear and colourless crystalline form of pure carbon, the hardest naturally occurring substance. Davy was the first to prove that diamond was pure carbon in Italy: 'Some Experiments on the Combustion of the Diamond and Other Carbonaceous Substances', Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society of London, Vol. 104 (1814), pp. 557-570.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Carbonic oxide

Symbol/formula: CO

Now known as carbon monoxide, a colourless, odourless, poisonous and flammable gas.

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.
