Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.

✕

Hornblende

A dark brown, black, or green mineral consisting of a silicate of calcium, magnesium, and iron, occurring in many igneous and metamorphic rocks.

✕

Mica

A shiny silicate mineral with a layered structure, found as minute scales in granite and other rocks, or as crystals.

✕

Feldspar

An abundant rock-forming mineral typically occurring as colourless or pale-coloured crystals and consisting of aluminosilicates of potassium, sodium, and calcium.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Sulfurous acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

Sulphuric acid. A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

Mount Somma

A mountain located in the Province of Naples, in the Campania region of southern Italy. It has a collapsed caldera of an older volcano, with Mount Vesuvius rising up from the center.

Pompeii

An ancient city located in what is now the comune of Pompei near Naples in the Campania region of Italy. Pompeii was buried in volcanic ash in the Eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 CE.
