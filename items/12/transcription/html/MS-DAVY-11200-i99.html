Geneva _ July 26. Qu?, Sunda Thursday
Made a series of expts on the actions of iodine
on the compounds of Phosphor s & Sulpr with chlorine
_ Some results will worthy of notice. _
1 st It is probable as I formerly suspected that there
is a gaseous compound of chlorine & phosphorus
3 of C. & 1 of phosphorus , & This dissolves |unites to| iodine
in enormous quantities & seems to form with it
an highly volatile triple compound of a deep
orange colour & which gives an orange gas __
- Sulphurane dissolves iodine & gives a purple
liquor which yeilds a purple vapour, & which
when acted on by water yeilds Sulpc Acid (probably)
& a whitish substance which seems to be a
compound of Sulphur & iodine , from which iodine
rose when it was heated & that in considerable
quantities.
- The Sublimate Phosphoranèe. became yellow
by the action of iodine . but had not that
striking effect upon it. which Phosphorane had
& the solution of phosphorus in Phosphorane
- When the water was added to the fluid
compound of phosphorus saturated with chlorine
& iodine the fluid became dissolved in it
& became deep orange & a brown powder
was precipitated which was afterwards redissolved.
- .All these expts worth further pursuit
Memm. to discover the name of Gazzaris
Banker. [xx] --- Lubbock

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

Phosphorus

Symbol/formula: P

A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO4

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

Phosphorane

Davy's name for a supposed compound of phosphorus.

JohnLubbock

Sir John Lubbock, 1st Baronet of Lammas, was a merchant, MP and prominent London banker. He helped establish and was a partner in the bank of Lemon, Buller, Finlay and Lubbock of London in 1772. He was also involved in diverse business ventures as a merchant.
