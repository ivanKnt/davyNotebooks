JohnDavy

Humphry Davy's brother, born in Penzance. Spent some time working with Davy in the Royal Institution before studying medicine at Edinburgh University. Appointed an Army physician he was elevated to the rank of Inspector General of Army Hospitals in the West Indies. Following his brother's death, Davy's notebooks came into his possession which he used in writing biographical studies that strongly defended his reputation. FRSL.

Sir JamesLowther

Sir James Lowther, 4th Baronet was an English landowner, industrialist and Whig politician who sat in the House of Commons for 54 years between 1694 and 1755. His ownership and development of coal mines around Whitehaven in Cumberland gave him substantial revenues, and he was reputed the richest commoner in England. He insituted many improvements in coal mining including the use of steam-driven pumps to drain mines and measures to try and combat firedamp.

River Wye

Strath Garve

Valley in the county of Ross and Cromarty, Scotland. Davy mentions 'Strathgarve' in his 'Salmonia; Or, Days of Fly Fishing': according to the voice of Poietes, 'the inn at Strathgarve is tolerable', although the sight of eagles near to the valley provides Halieus with 'a very fine and interesting sight'.
