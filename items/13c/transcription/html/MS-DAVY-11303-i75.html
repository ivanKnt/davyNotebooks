Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Iron(II) chloride

Symbol/formula: FeCl2

A chemical compound also known as ferrous chloride. The compound is white, but typical samples are often off-white.

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

eudiometrical

Relating to euidometry; the use of an euidometer: a graduated glass tube, closed at one end, that is used for measuring the change in the volume of gases during a chemical reaction.

LuigiGalvani

Italian surgeon and anatomist who carried out experiments with electricity and animals. The term 'galvanism' was named after him
