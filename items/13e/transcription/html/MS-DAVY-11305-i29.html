Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

In modern nomenclature, a mineral consisting of barium sulphate. In Davy's terminology it might have been equivalent to baryta, BaO.

✕

Phosphuret of strontian

Symbol/formula: Sr3P2

In modern terms this is strontium phosphide, an inorganic compound of strontium and phosphorus. The compound looks like black crystalline material.

✕

Strontian

Symbol/formula: Sr

Old terminology for strontium, an alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Boric acid

Symbol/formula: B(OH)3

A compound of boron, oxygen, and hydrogen. Davy often refers to this as boracic acid.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Phosphate of lime

Symbol/formula: Ca3(PO4)2

Tricalcium phosphate (sometimes abbreviated TCP) is a calcium salt of phosphoric acid.
