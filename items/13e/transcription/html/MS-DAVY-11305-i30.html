Sulphate of Strontian

Symbol/formula: SrSO4

The sulfate salt of strontium, in modern terms strontium sulphate.

✕

Barytes

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

Now known as barium sulphate (BaSO4), a white crystalline solid, occurring as the mineral barites.

✕

Muriate of barytes

Symbol/formula: BaCl2

Now known as barium chloride. It is a white powder, highly toxic, and imparts a yellow-green coloration to a flame.

✕

Strontian

Symbol/formula: Sr

Old terminology for strontium, an alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.
