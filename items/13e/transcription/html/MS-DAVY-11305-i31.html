ThomasBeddoes

A politically radical (Jacobin) chemist and physician who in the 1790s established the Medical Pneumatic Institution in Bristol to investigate the physiological and possible curative effects of all the gases discovered during the eighteenth century. Between late 1798 and early 1801 he employed Davy as Superintendent of the Institution.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Gaseous oxide of azote

Symbol/formula: N2O

Nitrous oxide. Davy initially refered to nitrous oxide as 'gaseous oxide of azote', but changed to 'nitrous oxide' on the discovery that the gas was respirable.

✕

Hydrocarbonate

Symbol/formula: H2 + CO

Hydrogen mixed with carbon monoxide.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.
