Eudiometer

A eudiometer is a laboratory device that measures the change in volume of a gas mixture following a physical or chemical change. An early version was used by Priestley in the discovery of oxygen.

✕

Pyrophorus

A substance the ignites spontaneously after exposure to air, one of the earliest known is that of Homberg.

✕

Iron(II) chloride

Symbol/formula: FeCl2

A chemical compound also known as ferrous chloride. The compound is white, but typical samples are often off-white.

✕

Phosphorus

Symbol/formula: P

A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.

✕

Liver of sulphur

A mixture of potassium sulphide, potassium polysulphide, potassium thiosulphate, and potassium bisulphide.

✕

Sulphite

Symbol/formula: SO

Any salt or ester of sulphurous acid, containing the ions SO32- or HSO3- (hydrogen sulphite) or the groups -SO3 or -HSO3. The salts are usually soluble crystalline compounds.
