Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

In modern nomenclature, a mineral consisting of barium sulphate. In Davy's terminology it might have been equivalent to baryta, BaO.

✕

Strontium

Symbol/formula: Sr

An alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Alumine

Symbol/formula: AL2O3

Alumine is an archaic name for alumina, which is aluminium oxide.

✕

Phosphoric acid

Symbol/formula: H3PO4

A corrosive acid that can form three different classes of salts, namely primary phosphates, dibasic phosphates, and tribasic phosphates. It is corrosive to ferrous metals and alloys.

✕

Boric acid

Symbol/formula: B(OH)3

A compound of boron, oxygen, and hydrogen. Davy often refers to this as boracic acid.

✕

Oxalic acid

Symbol/formula: (CO2H)2

An organic acid with the systematic name ethanedioic acid.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.
