The Réaumur scale

A temperature scale for which the melting and boiling points of water are defined as 0 and 80 degrees respectively.

✕

Reaumeur

a temperature scale, which Davy was converting to Fahrenheit.

✕

Daniel GabrielFahrenheit

German physicist, inventor, and scientific instrument maker, who invented the mercury-in-glass thermometer. The temperature scale he proposed is named after him. ForMemRSL.

✕

WilliamHenry

Manchester physician and chemist, whose Elements of Experimental Chemistry went through eleven editions. FRSL.

CharlesDesormes

Desormes was a French physicist and chemist. He did most of his scientific work in collaboration with his son-in-law, Nicolas Clément.

✕

NicolasClément

French chemist who worked with his father-in-law, Charles Desormes.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Muriate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCl2

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline solid at room temperature. Calcium chloride is highly soluble in water, and can be created by neutralising hydrochloric acid with calcium hydroxide.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.
