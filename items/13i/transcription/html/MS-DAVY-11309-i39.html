Made this day June 30 1810 -
a very curious set of
expts. - My object was,
to ascertain, if Oxygene
exists or can be detected
in oxymuriatic acid
-- - Now I took oxymuriate
Of Tin & exposed to Ammonia
the Ammonia was absorbed
without decomposition &
I attained, no oxide of
Tin , but a peculiar
volatile solid compound
consisting of Ammonia

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Oxymuriatic acid

Symbol/formula: Cl

Chlorine. Chlorine is a yellow-green gas at room temperature, with a pungent, irritating odour that is detectable at low concentrations.

✕

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.
