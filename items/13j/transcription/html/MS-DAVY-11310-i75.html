Cotopaxi

Cotopaxi is an active stratovolcano in the Andes Mountains, in Ecuador. The first European to climb it was Alexander Von Humboldt in 1802.

Quito

Quito, Ecuador's capital, sits high in the Andean foothills at an altitude of 2,850m. It is constructed on the foundations of an ancient Incan city.
