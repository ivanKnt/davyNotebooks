Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Fluorine

Symbol/formula: F

Carl Scheele’s investigations into fluorspar showed that it was a compound of calcium and an unidentified acid, capable of dissolving glass, released when fluorspar reacted with sulphuric acid. The unidentified substance was first shown to be an element in 1810 by André-Marie Ampère, and subsequently proven by Davy. The element came to be named fluorine through its commonly-known compound of fluorspar.

✕

Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Phosphorus

Symbol/formula: P

A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Boron

Symbol/formula: B

A non-metallic solid substance, believed to be a metal before it was isolated by Davy.

✕

Silicon

Symbol/formula: Si

After an attempt to isolate silicon in 1808, Davy proposed the name 'silicium' for silicon, from the Latin silex, silicis for flint, and adding the '-ium' ending because he believed it to be a metal.

✕

Strontium

Symbol/formula: Sr

An alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Barium

Symbol/formula: Ba

Davy was the first to isolate barium, using electrochemistry, in 1808. It is a soft, silvery alkaline earth metal, which due to its high chemical reactivity is never found in nature as a free element.

✕

Calcium

Symbol/formula: Ca

Davy was the first to isolate calcium, using electrochemistry, in 1808. It is a reactive metal that forms a dark oxide-nitride layer when exposed to air.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.
