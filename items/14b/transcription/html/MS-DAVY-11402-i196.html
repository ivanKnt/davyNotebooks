whole history of intellect is a history
of change, but change according to a
certain law; & we retain the memory
only of these changes which are useful
to us, or connected with our future
being. The child when born soon forgets
what happened to it in the womb; all
the recollections of the infant are lost,
because they would be useless to an
intellectual being; yet many of the
