Lord Byron . written
Whilst living . – 23.
Although thy youthful & [xxxx] |Wreath|,
capricious hands
Although thy youthful & luxuriant wreath
Of splendid & most glorious hues was woven
From all the fairest sweetest flowers of spring
Yet some strange blossoms & some poisonous weeds
Lovely in hue but from a torrid clime
When by the rose powerful sun
Were mingled with the jasmine & the rose
And the sweet orange flower
And thy dark locks

George GordonByron

From 1798 6th Lord Byron. Famous poet who knew both Humphry and Jane Davy in London and Italy; he wrote a slightly risqué skit on their marriage in 1812. FRSL.
