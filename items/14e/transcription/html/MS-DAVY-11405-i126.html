Once more I wet my feet with new delight
O Vandalis in thy translucent stream
Oft has thou been to me a lovely dream
Blue in thy stillness in thy anger white.-

Zephyr

Greek mythology: god of the west wind. His ame is sometimes used to describe a breeze that is gentle or westerly.

Venus

a Roman goddess, whose functions encompass love, beauty, desire, sex, fertility, prosperity, and victory

Wandle

A tributary of the River Thames in south London, England. Davy writes a poem 'To the Wandle, or Vandalis' (notebook 14E, pp. 122-1) and writes about the river in Samonia.
