These poem |verses| was written by one
Inspruck July 25 1827.
of the |parts| on the fall of the Traun .
From the high rock thy lovely
waters burst
As if a new creation, like the stream
That in the desert from | gushed | the |hallowed| powerful
wand
Of Israels mighty prophet sprung to life
To save his people.. But the dreamy thought
Of that most blessed though but scanty rill
Gives no idea of thy might & power
And awful force & fulness. As of a spirit
Imprisoned by magic art & now released
Thou thunderst on determined to destroy
And thy mild functions to produce & cheer
Are changed for attributes more terrible
Saddening & destructive - carrying onward on
|Rocks| Trees before thee e'en the grat pine.-
Rending the rock, leaving the mountain side |mountain & through a new torn vale|
And Opening herself a passage to the plain
