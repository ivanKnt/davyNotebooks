On the death of Lord Byron
composed at Westhill in the
great storm, Novr. 1824
1
Gone is the Bard, who like a powerful
spirit
A beautiful but fallen child of light
Of fiery seraphs, the aspiring peer
Seemed fitted by his nature to inherit
A wilder state than that the genial
strife
Oh mighty Elements have given our sphere
Fixed in a stated round its course to
run
A chained Slave around the master sun
2
Of some great comet He might well
have been
The habitant that through the mighty
space
Of kindling ether rolls, now visiting
Our little world |glorious sun| By wondering myriads seen
