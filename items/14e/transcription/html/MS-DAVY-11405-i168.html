E'en with respect to human things & forms
We estimate & know them but
In solitude. The eye of the worldly Man
Is insect like
Fit only for the near & single object
The true philosophers at in distance sees them
And scans their forms their bearings & relations
To view a lovely lanscape in its whole
We do not fix upon a single tree or rock
Or wooded hill out of the range
Of the whole scenery. We rather mount
A distant lofty knoll & mark the varied whole
The waters blue, the mountains grey & dim
The shaggy hills & the embattled cliffs
With their mysterious glens awakening
Imaginations wild & interminable
