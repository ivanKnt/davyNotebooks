49
Dissolves to vivify the parched earth.
And whether
And I have not unkind
both coldness & unkindness
As the |fair plant of Ceylon| cingalian tree which wounded |when cut.|
|Does| not alone perfume the axe: but gives a balmy
oil

Which preserves | its harsh & sullen
texture from decay. –

Eagles.
The mighty birds still onwards |upwards| rose
In slow but constant |& most steady| flight
The young ones following. & they would pause
As if to teach them how to bear the
light
And keep the solar glory full
in sight.
So went they on till from excess
of pain
