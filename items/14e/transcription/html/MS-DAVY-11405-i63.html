Like the mild Zephyr of the full born |spring|
Succeeding to the frosty northern blasts
She felt that nature had designed her |to perform|
All soft and tender duties; to become
A Wife, a Mother: that her heart was |formed|
Not for the dull inert and callous round
Of earthly |courtly forms and| ceremonies; but soft & filled
With spirit |power| and with passion to become
All natural sympathies; to interweave
Itself with other hearts; to glow with |rapture|
At anothers joys; to melt in sorrows
At anothers woes: This was its Spring |time|
After its winter frost;
A helpless innocent condemned to die
Gently flowed on the water as
The sun shone on them in full brightness
