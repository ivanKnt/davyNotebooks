Aug 1 1819
He who passes from flower to flower
& from fruit to fruit enjoying their
fragrance & their sweets may
sometimes be pricked by the thorns
round the rose or stung by a
wasp on the peach: but the
anguish is transient as the cause
He is truly wretched who having
all his life raised one flower
& cultivated one fruit finds the
fragrance & the tints destroyed by
a frost & the thorns alone remaining
& the fruit fair & fresh without
but with the worm rottenness
& corruption within.
