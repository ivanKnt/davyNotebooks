He can compare the pleasure
He derives from the eye with that
He derives from the ear & the palate
Who is there that would not give smell
more |rather than| taste |& taste| rather than hearing &
hearing rather than seeing, &
if the alternative would be imagined
possible of his giving up the ideal
or intellectual life the life of light
& imagination rather than |or that a| that
of appetite & sense, He would
not hesitate to prefer mind the
life of imagination, hope &
desire to that of mere
sensation mere organic
feelings & movements. - -
