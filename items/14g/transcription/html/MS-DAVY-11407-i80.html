Feldspar

An abundant rock-forming mineral typically occurring as colourless or pale-coloured crystals and consisting of aluminosilicates of potassium, sodium, and calcium.

Mica

A shiny silicate mineral with a layered structure, found as minute scales in granite and other rocks, or as crystals.

✕

Zeolite

Any member of a family of hydrated aluminosilicate minerals that contain alkali and alkaline-earth metals.

Olivine

Augite

The most common pyroxene mineral (a silicate of calcium, magnesium, iron, titanium, and aluminum).

Tourmaline

Tourmaline is a boron silicate. Opaque and black in colour, tourmaline is a semi-precious stone that can be found in a variety of colours based on the presence of iron, magnesium, or lithium. Boron was first isolated by Davy in 1808.
