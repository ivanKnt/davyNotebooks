Platinum

Symbol/formula: Pt

A dense, malleable, ductile, highly unreactive, precious, silverish-white transition metal.

✕

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

✕

Camphor

Symbol/formula: C10H16O

A chemical made by distilling the wood and bark of the camphor tree. Commonly used in lotions and creams to relieve pain and irritation

✕

Elastic fluid

A descriptive term for a gas, emphasising its elastic quality of spontaneous expansion.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.

✕

Antimony

Symbol/formula: Sb

A lustrous gray metalloid, it is found in nature mainly as the sulphide mineral stibnite. Antimony compounds have been known since ancient times and were powdered for use as medicine and cosmetics, often known by the Arabic name 'kohl'.

✕

Tellurium

Symbol/formula: Te

A brittle, mildly toxic, rare, silver-white metalloid. Tellurium is chemically related to selenium and sulphur, all three of which are chalcogens. It is occasionally found in its native form as elemental crystals.

✕

Arsenic

Symbol/formula: As

Arsenic occurs in many minerals, usually in combination with sulphur and metals, but also as a pure elemental crystal. Arsenic is a metalloid.

✕

Sulfurous acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

Sulphuric acid. A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen.

✕

Prussic acid

Symbol/formula: HCN

Hydrogen cyanide. A colourless, extremely poisonous, and flammable liquid that boils slightly above room temperature.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.
