Iodine

Symbol/formula: I

An element and the heaviest of the group of stable halogens. Bernard Courtois was the first to isolate the substance. Whilst in Paris Davy investigated the substance, and proved it to be a chemical element describing his results in a paper sent to the Royal Society. This caused disputes between Davy and Joseph Louis Gay-Lussac, who had also been researching the element.

✕

Hyperoxymuriate of potassa

Symbol/formula: KC103

Hyperoxymuriate of potassa was later known as chlorate of potassa. It is a salt now more commonly known as potassium chlorate.

✕

Chlorionic acid

In his paper 'Some Experiments on a Solid Compound of Iodine and Oxygene, and on Its Chemical Agencies', published for the Royal Society in 1815, Davy described chlorionic acid as 'the substance produced by the agency of
the combination of iodine and chlorine in water' (Vol 105, 1815).

✕

Chlorine

Symbol/formula: Cl

Davy named chlorine in 1810 and insisted it was an element and not a compound.

✕

Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.

✕

Hornblende

A dark brown, black, or green mineral consisting of a silicate of calcium, magnesium, and iron, occurring in many igneous and metamorphic rocks.

✕

Yesterday March 16th - ascended Vesuvius

Davy's account differs from Faraday's; the latter writes in his journal that they ascended on 13th and 14th May, see B. Jones, The life and letters of Faraday, i. 132 and 136.

✕

Mount Somma

A mountain located in the Province of Naples, in the Campania region of southern Italy. It has a collapsed caldera of an older volcano, with Mount Vesuvius rising up from the center.
