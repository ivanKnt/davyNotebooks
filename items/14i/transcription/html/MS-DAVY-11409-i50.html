Arago like a Spaniard in Countenance had a little
of the reserve & dignity of manner belonging to that
nation. He was I believe from Rompilieu or the neighborhood
of the pyryenees. He appeared to me candid in judgement
& accurate as an observer & experimenter. He was
almost constantly in discussion with Biot upon some
points of Scontested discovery & I felt always disposed
to think him in the right. He seemed to me to
have so much more steadiness of character. Biot
was mobile active ingenious & laborious; but had
a restlessness not free from |obvious| vanity Madame Arago
was a handsome Woman & very agreable |without pretensions| & Madame
Biot was lively & agreeable. – I cannot say of
Madame Biot that she was without pretensions but

Dominique François JeanArago

French mathematician, physicist, astronomer and political liberal. Davy presented him with the Copley Medal in 1825. ForMemRSL.

GabrielleBiot

née Brisson; she was only sixteen when they married in 1797. She was already fluent in German and Biot taught her mathemathics and physics so that she could translate into French a German text by Ernst Gottfried Fischer. They had a son, Édouard Constant Biot, who was born in 1803.

ThomasBeddoes

A politically radical (Jacobin) chemist and physician who in the 1790s established the Medical Pneumatic Institution in Bristol to investigate the physiological and possible curative effects of all the gases discovered during the eighteenth century. Between late 1798 and early 1801 he employed Davy as Superintendent of the Institution.
