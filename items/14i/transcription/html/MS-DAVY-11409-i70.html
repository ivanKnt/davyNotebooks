by that possessed by Schumacher . I was
equally pleased with the manners - with the
liberality & social gaiety of these three
celebrated men with whom I spent one of
the most agreeable days belonging to the later

Hans ChristianØrsted

Danish physicist and chemist who won the Copley Medal in 1820 for his electro-chemical experiments and discoveries; he had become a professor at the University of Copenhagen in 1806.

Carl FriedrichGauss

Prominent Hanoverian mathematician who was Director of the observatory at Gottingen University. Davy met him during a visit to Bremen in 1824. ForMemRSL.

HeinrichOlbers

Astronomer who worked in Bremen. He specialised in the detection of asteroids and propounded his eponymous paradox. Davy met him on a visit to Bremen in 1824. ForMemRSL.
