Frederick WilliamHerschel

Hanoverian astronomer and composer who while living in Bath discovered the planet later named Uranus. Moving to Slough he built the world's largest telescope to date which Davy visited when he was courting Jane Apreece. FRSL.

✕

EdwardJenner

Lived in Berkeley, Gloucestershire, where he pioneered vacination, especially for smallpox. FRSL.

✕

MatthewBaillie

Physician and brother of Joanna Baillie. One of the doctors who saw Davy during his serious illness from late 1807 to early 1808. FRSL.

✕

Alexander John GaspardMarcet

Genevean-born physician who settled in London and lectured on chemistry at Guy's Hospital. A friend of Davy's. FRSL.

✕

André-MarieAmpère

French physicist and mathematician and one of the key figure in the early study of electro-magnetism. Davy met him in Paris in 1813 and they developed a scientific rivalry. ForMemRSL.

hyppogrif

a legendary animal that has the foreparts of a winged griffin and the body and hindquarters of a horse. The creature was invented by Ludovico Ariosto in his Orlando furioso and was based on a proverbial phrase about crossing a griffin with a horse that was used to signify an impossibility or incongruity.
