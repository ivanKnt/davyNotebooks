JaneDavy

Daughter of Charles Kerr, an Antiguan merchant who made his fortune in highly dubious circumstances. Following his death she moved to Britain where she married Schukbrugh Apreece who died in 1807. After running a successful literary salon in Edinburgh she moved to London where she met Davy and they married on 11 April 1812 two days after he had been knighted by the Prince Regent.

✕

JohnMurray

Publisher of many of the key literary and scientific texts of the time, including by Byron, Austen, Davy. Published The Quarterly Journal of Science (closely likned to the Royal Institution) as well as Davy's Six Discourses (1827), Salmonia (1828) and Consolations in Travel (1830).

✕

NickolasEsterhazy

Hungarian prince and patron of the arts, especially music. Davy presented him with a copy of Salmonia.
