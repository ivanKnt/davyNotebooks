EdwardJenner

Lived in Berkeley, Gloucestershire, where he pioneered vacination, especially for smallpox. FRSL.

✕

MatthewBaillie

Physician and brother of Joanna Baillie. One of the doctors who saw Davy during his serious illness from late 1807 to early 1808. FRSL.

✕

Alexander John GaspardMarcet

Genevean-born physician who settled in London and lectured on chemistry at Guy's Hospital. A friend of Davy's. FRSL.

✕

André-MarieAmpère

French physicist and mathematician and one of the key figure in the early study of electro-magnetism. Davy met him in Paris in 1813 and they developed a scientific rivalry. ForMemRSL.

✕

JosephPriestley

Unitarian and political radical forced into exile in Pennsylvania. As a natural philospher and chemist who retained belief in phlogiston, he discovered oxygen and nitrous oxide though not under those names. FRSL.

✕

JamesWoodhouse

Physician and professor of chemistry at University of Pennslyvania from 1795. Visited London in 1802 where he met Davy and later repeated his experiments on nitrous oxide.
