Sanctity on Etna

The sanctity on Etna may refer to a story told at the end of Dialogue III of Consolations in Travel "of a very ingenious geological philosopher now living" (possibly |Sir James Hall) who acquired the support of the initially suspicious peasants by claiming that he had made a religious vow to carry away with him pieces of every kind of stone found upon the mountain. They called him "the saint", and with their religiously motivated support he could make an ample collection of geological specimens from Etna. (Dialogue III, page 312)

Mount Etna
