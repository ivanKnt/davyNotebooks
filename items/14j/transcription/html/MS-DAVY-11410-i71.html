In Youth - in health in
prosperity Man has [x] little
occasion for & seldom refers
to the consolations |effects| of religion
Which at such times visits
the heart rather as aspirations
of grati feelings |emotions| of gratitude
& sublime love - than
as feelings of submission
faith & humble trust
in the divine Will. but
in misfortune, in sickness
in old age then it is
that the religious feeling
has its great & true effect
then it comes as consolation
& as hope Then it creates
powers which appear to be
as a divine spirit of
light in the darkness |extinct & gives a freshness|
of immat youth which was supposed
of mortal life Then
to have passed away for ever by
it is the Pharos guiding
the mind but which is now rendered
a youth of immortality
