There tranquilly & more agreeably
than in the metropolis of
my own Country Nature never
deceives us, the Rocks the
mountains, the streams always speak |the the same language|
You I know said
unknown are a dreamer
of dreams but do
not identify me with
the genius of your vision
in the Collosseum .
I have no claims to
superiority of character
or intellect, I have
accidentally done you a
service & I am rejoiced at
it as it enables me
to renew an acquaintance
from which I expect
much pleasure

The Unknown

A fictional character in Humphry Davy's Consolations in Travel (1830).
