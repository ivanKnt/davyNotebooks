thermometer in the shade [...] moistened bulb.

Comparing the temperatures indicated by a normal ("dry") thermometer and one with a moistenend bulb ("wet bulb thermometer") allows to determine the humidity content of the air. The lower the humidity, the higher the evaporation rate from the wet bulb, and the stronger the evaporative cooling effect. The relative humidity of the ambient air can be computed from these two temperatures, in this case it was 53 %.
