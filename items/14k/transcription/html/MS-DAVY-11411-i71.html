temperature in NW [...] still 55.2

Comparing the temperatures shown by a thermometer with a dry bulb and one with a wetted bulb was and still is a method of measuring the humidity content of the air. The evaporation cools the wet thermometer, and since the evaporation rate depends on the vapour content of the air, the temperature difference allows to determine the humidity. The evaporation-induced temperature difference of "2° only" reported by Davy indicates high humidity, in this case the relative humidity can be estimated as about 88 % and so was very high indeed.
