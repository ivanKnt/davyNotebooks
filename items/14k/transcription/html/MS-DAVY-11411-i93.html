Thermometer at 4 68 Moist 56

Comparing the temperatures shown by a thermometer with a dry bulb and one with a wetted bulb was and still is a method of measuring the humidity content of the air. The evaporation cools the wet thermometer, and since the evaporation rate depends on the vapour content of the air, the temperature difference allows to determine the humidity. Dry temperature 68°, moist temperature 56° ≈ 47% rel. humidity.
