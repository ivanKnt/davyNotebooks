Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Greywacke

Greywacke or graywacke is a type of sandstone.

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Pyrites

A generic term for sulphide minerals.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Lakes of Killarney

Three lakes (Lough Leane, Muckross Lake - also called Middle Lake, and Upper Lake) surrounded by the MacGillycuddy's Reeks and now part of Killarney National Park.

✕

Edgeworthstown

Edgeworthstown is a town in County Longford, Ireland. It is named after the Edgeworth family, who lived on an estate there. It was the home of Richard Lovell Edgeworth, Associate at the Medical Pneumatic Institution, and his daughter Anna Beddoes, née Edgeworth.
