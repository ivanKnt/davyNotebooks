Herschels planet

William Herschel (1738-1822) was an astronomer and composer, and in 1781 he was the first to report sightings of what he identified to be a new planet, later called Uranus. John Frederick William Herschel (1792-1871), William's son, was a close aquaintance of Davy's and Davy helped to foster his career while Davy was president of the Royal Society.
