Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Crow/Cat Coal

In Yorkshire "cat coal" is a term for coal containing pyrites; "crow coal" was a term used sometimes for anthracite due to its shiny black appearance.

✕

Silicious

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Siliceous rocks: any of a group of sedimentary rocks that consist largely or almost entirely of silicon dioxide.

✕

Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.

✕

Hazel

In some mining areas ‘hazel’ was an old mining term for sandstone.

✕

Shiver

A regional mining term for rock which is "shivery" or easily broken up.
