Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.

✕

Hornblende

A dark brown, black, or green mineral consisting of a silicate of calcium, magnesium, and iron, occurring in many igneous and metamorphic rocks.

✕

Galena

Symbol/formula: PbS

A bluish, grey, or black mineral of metallic appearance, consisting of lead sulphide. It is the chief ore of lead.

✕

Calcite

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcite is a carbonate mineral and the most stable polymorph of calcium carbonate. Davy refers to it as Calcareous Spar.

✕

Quartz

Symbol/formula: SiO2

A hard, crystalline mineral composed of silica (silicon dioxide).

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Gangue

In mining, gangue is the commercially worthless material that surrounds, or is closely mixed with, a wanted mineral in an ore deposit

✕

Dike

A dike or dyke is a sheet of rock that is formed in a fracture of a pre-existing rock body. Dikes can be either magmatic or sedimentary in origin.
