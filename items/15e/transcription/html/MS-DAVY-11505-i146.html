✕

Whinstone

A term used to describe any hard dark-coloured rock, examples of which include igneous rocks and basalt.

✕

Quartz

Symbol/formula: SiO2

A hard, crystalline mineral composed of silica (silicon dioxide).

✕

Granite

A coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock.

✕

Gossan

Gossan (eiserner hut or eisenhut) is intensely oxidized, weathered or decomposed rock, usually the upper and exposed part of an ore deposit or mineral vein. As noted by Davy, its name comes from Cornish mining slang.

✕

Barytes

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

Now known as barium sulphate (BaSO4), a white crystalline solid, occurring as the mineral barites.

✕

Chlorite

Green hydrous silicates of magnesia and alumina occuring in ancient rock formations.

✕

Gangue

In mining, gangue is the commercially worthless material that surrounds, or is closely mixed with, a wanted mineral in an ore deposit
