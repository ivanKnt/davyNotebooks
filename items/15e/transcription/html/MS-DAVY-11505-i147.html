Whinstone

A term used to describe any hard dark-coloured rock, examples of which include igneous rocks and basalt.

✕

Lead

Symbol/formula: Pb

A heavy metal that is denser than most common materials. Lead is soft and malleable, and also has a relatively low melting point.

✕

Strontian

The main village in Sunart, an area in western Lochaber, in the Highlands of Scotland. It was where the mineral strontianite was discovered, and from which the element strontium was first isolated.

✕

Gangue

In mining, gangue is the commercially worthless material that surrounds, or is closely mixed with, a wanted mineral in an ore deposit

✕

Dike

A dike or dyke is a sheet of rock that is formed in a fracture of a pre-existing rock body. Dikes can be either magmatic or sedimentary in origin.
