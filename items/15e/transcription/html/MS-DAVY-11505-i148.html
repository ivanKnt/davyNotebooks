Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.

✕

Iron

Symbol/formula: Fe

A strong, hard, magnetic silvery-grey metal. It is, by mass, the most common element on Earth, just ahead of oxygen, forming much of Earth's outer and inner core.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Bitumen

A sticky, black, highly viscous liquid or semi-solid form of petroleum.

✕

Micaceous shist

A rock composed of mica and quartz.

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.
