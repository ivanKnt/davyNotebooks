The Isle of Mull is the second-largest island of the Inner Hebrides (after Skye) and lies off the west coast of Scotland.

Granite

A coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock.

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Jade

Jade is an umbrella term for two different types of decorative rocks used for jewelry or ornaments. Jade is often referred to by either of two different silicate mineral names: nephrite (a silicate of calcium and magnesium in the amphibole group of minerals), or jadeite (a silicate of sodium and aluminum in the pyroxene group of minerals).

✕

Asbestos

A group of naturally occurring fibrous minerals that are resistant to heat and corrosion.

✕

Dike

A dike or dyke is a sheet of rock that is formed in a fracture of a pre-existing rock body. Dikes can be either magmatic or sedimentary in origin.
