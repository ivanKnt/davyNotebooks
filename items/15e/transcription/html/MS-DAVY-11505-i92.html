To attempt to explain all
the vague opinions which then
have adopted would be a
useless labour, to draw
Mystery out of Mystery is
of little use, Of what avail
were the elaborate disquisitions
of Porphyry the great
Apologist for the pagan
superstitions – He thought
He could prove that
the worship of the
basaltic idols of [x]
or black stones signifies
[xx] that the divine
Nature is imperceptible

Porphyry

A Neoplatonic philosopher born in Tyre, Roman Phoenicia during Roman rule.

✕

Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.
