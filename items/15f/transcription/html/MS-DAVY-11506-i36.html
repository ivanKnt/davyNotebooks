Plutonic Theory

Relates to the Plutonist theories of James Hutton (1715-1795), who proposed that the earth's igneous rocks were formed from magma.

✕

Neptunian Theory

A theory of geology proposed by Abraham Gottlob Werner, who proposed that rocks formed from the crystallisation of minerals in the early Earth's oceans.

✕

fills imbeded in

The word ‘fills’ has geological relevance – related to cavities and fissures in rocks, also called vugs (a Cornish word)

✕

Pyrites

A generic term for sulphide minerals.

✕

Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.
