✕

ThomasBernard

Philantropist and a founding member of the Royal Institution serving as an early Treasurer. Later influential in running the institution, especially its lecture programme.

✕

SamuelBoddington

Member of Parliament for Tralee in 1807 with a large estate in the West Indies. In February 1804, he gave the Institution a mineral collection which Davy valued at £150.

✕

WilliamBabington

Physician to Guy's Hospital and mineralogist, one of the founders of the Geological Society. A close friend of Davy, Babington was one of those who treated him during his severe illness of late 1807, early 1808. Shared his enthusiasm for angling. FRSL.

✕

ThomasDouglas

Lord Selkirk was Thomas Douglas, from 1799, 5th Earl of Selkirk (1771-1820), an Annual Subscriber to the Royal Institution and involved with the Board of Agriculture. He was heavily involved in the Highland clearances moving people to Canada. FRSL from 1808.

✕

HowePeter Browne

The Earl of Altamont was the courtesy title of Howe Peter Browne, from 1809 2nd Marquis of Sligo, He was an Annual Subscriber to the Royal Institution and (much) later Governor of Jamacia.

✕

MaryHill

Lady Downshire was Mary Hill, nee Sandys, Marchioness of Downshire and later a Baroness in her own right. Her husband Arthur Hill, 2nd Marquess of Downshire, was involved in the Board of Agriculture.
