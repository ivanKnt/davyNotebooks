Friday Rickman
Saty Mr Cripps
Sunday Mr Bernard
[xx] next
Wedy Evg Mrs Young
Thursday Mr Boddington

✕

Thomas (Tom)Wedgwood

Youngest son of Josiah Wedgwood who became a close friend of Davy's. Together they published a paper which has been taken as marking the start of chemical photography.

✕

Thomas CharlesHope

Professor of Medicine and Chemistry at Edinburgh University. Discovered the element strontium and seems to have played a role in Davy's appointment to the Royal Institution. FRSL.

✕

GraceDavy

Davy's mother who spent her entire life in West Cornwall.

✕

AnnaBeddoes

Anna Beddoes, née Edgeworth, the wife of Thomas Beddoes who befriended Davy when he arrived in Bristol. Critics have speculated that they had an affair because Davy wrote poems to Anna and she wrote poems for him that he copied out in his notebooks.

✕

JohnRickman

FRS, a civil servant and statistician. He was a close friend of Robert Southey.

✕

CrippsJohn Martyn

Sussex landowner, annual subscriber to the RI and honorary member of the Board of Agriculture.

✕

ThomasBernard

Philantropist and a founding member of the Royal Institution serving as an early Treasurer. Later influential in running the institution, especially its lecture programme.

✕

MarthaAllen

Arthur Young's wife and Charles Burney's sister-in-law. Her marriage was apparently unhappy. She was the the great-grand-daughter of John Allen, and daughter of Alderman Allen, of Lyng House in the county of Norfolk. Her mother was Mary Maxey, (c.1705-1776).

✕

SamuelBoddington

Member of Parliament for Tralee in 1807 with a large estate in the West Indies. In February 1804, he gave the Institution a mineral collection which Davy valued at £150.
