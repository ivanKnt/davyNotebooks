Feldspar

An abundant rock-forming mineral typically occurring as colourless or pale-coloured crystals and consisting of aluminosilicates of potassium, sodium, and calcium.

Trass

A grey or cream-coloured fragmental rock.

Porphyry

A hard, igneous purplish-red rock in which crystals of feldspar are embedded in a fine-grained groundmass.

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Breccia

A rock composed of large angular broken fragments of minerals or rocks cemented together by a fine-grained matrix.

✕

Sienite

Syenite. A coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock with a general composition similar to that of granite, but deficient in quartz, which, if present at all, occurs in relatively small concentrations.

✕

Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.

✕

Shale

Shale is a fine-grained, clastic sedimentary rock formed from mud that is a mix of flakes of clay minerals and tiny fragments of other minerals, especially quartz and calcite.
