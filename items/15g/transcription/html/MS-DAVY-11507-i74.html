Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.

✕

Zeolite

Any member of a family of hydrated aluminosilicate minerals that contain alkali and alkaline-earth metals.

✕

Quartz

Symbol/formula: SiO2

A hard, crystalline mineral composed of silica (silicon dioxide).

✕

Porphyry

A hard, igneous purplish-red rock in which crystals of feldspar are embedded in a fine-grained groundmass.

✕

Feldspar

An abundant rock-forming mineral typically occurring as colourless or pale-coloured crystals and consisting of aluminosilicates of potassium, sodium, and calcium.

✕

Pitchstone

A dark coloured, glassy volcanic rock formed when felsic lava or magma cools quickly.

✕

Sandy Braes

Perhaps part of the Tardree Mountain complex, County Antrim, Northern Ireland, a volcanic vent complex with very rare rocks, which belong to the Tardree Rhyolite Complex, or Sallagh Braes, a mountain in County Antrim.
