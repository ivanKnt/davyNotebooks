Terra japonica

Terra japonica, or Extractum catechu, is an extract of acacia. It is used in India and Malyasia for dyeing and tanning. This appears to be a reference to Davy's experimentation into its properties.

✕

lixiviation

The process of a solute becoming detached from its carrier substance by way of a solvent.

✕

Mimosa

Davy is referring to Acacia trees, particularly Acacia catechu, which are no longer classified as belonging to the mimosa genus.

JosephBanks

A member of the Lincolnshire gentry, Banks sailed as a botanist on James Cook's first voyage to the Pacific (1768-1771). Elected President of the Royal Society of London in 1778 he served for forty-two years until just before his death. The dominant figure in the running of English science during that time, he became one of Davy's patrons and ultimately Davy succeeded him to the Presidency. FRSL.
