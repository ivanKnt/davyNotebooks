Gallic acid

Symbol/formula: C7H6O5

A crystalline acid prepared from the oak-gall and other vegetable products, used as a tanning agent.

✕

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

✕

Muriate of tin

Symbol/formula: SnCl4

Stannic chloride. Tin chloride is a white crystalline solid. It forms a stable dihydrate, but aqueous solutions tend to undergo hydrolysis, particularly if hot.

✕

Carbonate of potash

Symbol/formula: K2CO3

Potassium carbonate. It is a white salt, which is soluble in water and forms a strongly alkaline solution.

Joseph LouisProust

French analytical chemist. Author of the 1794 paper 'Researches on Prussian Blue', in which he first set down the law of definite proportions, or Proust's law, as it is commonly known.

Tannin

Biiter and astringent compounds found in plants. They include Ellagic acid, gallic acid, and pyrogallic acid which were first discovered by Braconnot in 1831.
