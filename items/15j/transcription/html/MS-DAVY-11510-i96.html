Litmus

A water-soluble mixture of different dyes extracted from lichens used to test materials for acidity.

✕

Nitrate of mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg(NO3)2

Mercury(II) nitrate. Mercury nitrate salts, which are colourless or white and soluble, are occasionally used as a reagent. It is made by treating mercury with hot concentrated nitric acid.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Silver nitrate

Symbol/formula: AgNO3

Silver is dissolved in nitric acid and this solution is then evaporated to leave a white crystalline solid.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Muriate of tin

Symbol/formula: SnCl4

Stannic chloride. Tin chloride is a white crystalline solid. It forms a stable dihydrate, but aqueous solutions tend to undergo hydrolysis, particularly if hot.

✕

Tannin

Biiter and astringent compounds found in plants. They include Ellagic acid, gallic acid, and pyrogallic acid which were first discovered by Braconnot in 1831.
