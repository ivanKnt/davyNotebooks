It had been observed in the earliest expts on
the Voltaic pile that Soda was protruded
from the cloths where they were in contact
with the silver; All the experiments
likewise shewed that - saline matters
were decomposed in the circuit; & that
inflammable matters in general were determined
towards the negative surface;- Thus I found
that Sulphur , from& the metals even those supposed
incapable of being revived by nascent Hydrogene
followed the same law - & Mr Mess Bergelius
& Pontin &. shewed that saline solutions
in general; were decomposed according to the
same circumstances as muriate of Soda .
in a series of extensive expts made to
investigate the laws of these changes,
I found that all compounds whether
[xxxxxxxxx] soluble or insoluble, whether
saline or stoney. afforded their acid
Matter at the positive surface &
their alkaline Matter at the negative surface.

Voltaic

Refers to the current generated by voltaic pile, invented by Italian chemist Alessandro Volta.

✕

Saline

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Sodium chloride in solution.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.
