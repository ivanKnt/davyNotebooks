✕

Mica

A shiny silicate mineral with a layered structure, found as minute scales in granite and other rocks, or as crystals.

✕

Silicious

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Siliceous rocks: any of a group of sedimentary rocks that consist largely or almost entirely of silicon dioxide.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Hornblende

A dark brown, black, or green mineral consisting of a silicate of calcium, magnesium, and iron, occurring in many igneous and metamorphic rocks.

✕

Schorl

A form of tourmaline, a crystalline silicate mineral group in which boron is compounded with elements such as aluminium, iron, magnesium, sodium, lithium, or potassium.

✕

Serpentine

A metamorphic rock containing one or more of the serpentine group minerals.

✕

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Elastic fluid

A descriptive term for a gas, emphasising its elastic quality of spontaneous expansion.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Iron oxide

Symbol/formula: Fe2O3

Oxide of iron. A dark red solid which occurs as the mineral haematite and as the main component of rust.
