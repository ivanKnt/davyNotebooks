The different primitive Rocks have very
different modes of junction and of transition
into each other.-
In some cases the parts of their union
one in right lines a circumstance which
often occurs in the meeting of silicious
Shist and Granite and porphyry -
Instance
Serpentine Sientite and micaceous shist
sometimes pass into each other by very
gentle gradations
And where granular Limestone is placed
upon micaceous shist near the point
of junction it uniformly contains larger
or smaller proportions of Mica -

Silicious

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Siliceous rocks: any of a group of sedimentary rocks that consist largely or almost entirely of silicon dioxide.

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Granite

A coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock.

✕

Micaceous shist

A rock composed of mica and quartz.

✕

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Mica

A shiny silicate mineral with a layered structure, found as minute scales in granite and other rocks, or as crystals.
