Granite and micaceous shist sometimes have
their parts mixed at the point of junction
and in other cases they inosculate sending
forth distinct ramifications of Veins.
Instance
This fact occurs on a considerable scale at
St Michaels Mount in Cornwall ;- The great
body of the mountain is of Granite ; but at
its base a stratum of the Shist occurs:
and this layer of Granite may be traced
to a considerable extent running into the
Shist . whilst. ramifications of the Shist
in many places penetrate into the Granite
Those in|oscu|lations of Granite and Shist
which occur abundantly in different
parts of the world have been subjects of
great disputes

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

inosculations

Inosculation is a natural phenomenon in which trunks, branches or roots of two trees grow together in a manner biologically similar to the artificial process of grafting.
