Besides the great series of columns there are
several smaler rows round the mouth of
the cave placed one above the other and
the hollows in the lower columns are
fitted to receive the proportions of the upper
columns -
this is a part of one of the small series
of columns which I brought from Staffa
x last summer - I had the satisfaction of
passing a day upon the Island, my expectations
were raised to the highest pitch with regard
to Fingals cave by the descriptions of
Sir Jos Banks Dr Van Troil and the Honur
Mrs yet the scene exalted the highest
sentiment of admiration.

JosephBanks

A member of the Lincolnshire gentry, Banks sailed as a botanist on James Cook's first voyage to the Pacific (1768-1771). Elected President of the Royal Society of London in 1778 he served for forty-two years until just before his death. The dominant figure in the running of English science during that time, he became one of Davy's patrons and ultimately Davy succeeded him to the Presidency. FRSL.
