The relations of the positions of the different
secondary Rocks that have been described are
almost as diversified as those of primitive
rocks and vary in particular districts
Limestone is the secondary rock generally
discovered at the greatest depth and Shist
or Sandstone are usually found placed
immediately upon it;

✕

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.
