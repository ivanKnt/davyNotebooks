but hardly ever under it;
And the strata commonly above coal are
coa sand stone and loose Shist and
it sometimes occurs beneath Basalt -
Instance
In Derbyshire where the surface has been
penetrated to more than 1500 feet upon
3 regular strata only have been found
of these the first is Sandstone the second
brown Limestone the third Shist the
fourth coal the fifth Sandstone the sixth
black Limestone and the seventh
grey Shell Limestone -

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.

✕

Shell limestone

A type of limestone composed largely of shells.

Derbyshire

Derbyshire is a county in the East Midlands and includes part of the Peak District.
