Lucretius appears |seems| to allude to it in
his second book when he speaks of the
various forms of shells -
And ovid in the fifteenth book of
metamorphoses expresses the notion distinctly
in some lines of which I shall read
a translation
Remains that to the waters owe their birth
Ocean in Rocks beneath the solid earth
Where our Green land their varied face display
Once in proud triumph flowed the azure sea
And in the cange |change| of things and lapse of time
The conquering waves have gained another clime
And where another land its verdure spread
So how the moving oceans troubled fled.
