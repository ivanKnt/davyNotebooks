difficult to assign any satisfactory cause for
their formation -

by fusion is evident for if their constituent
parts are at all capable of being rendered
liquid by Heat it can only be by a degree
much more intense than we are able to
produce; and such a heat acting under
pressure must have necessarily have likewise
fused The calcareous matter surrounding them
