Where wood has been buried in the Earth
in late times |_ from the action of
water and of air it always undergoes
a change which brings it to a state
similar to that of Pit Coal -
At Bovey in Devonshire a whole forest
is found buried under different strata of
clay and gravel and the trees though
Carbonaceous and black have their ligneous
form and contain a substance apparently
intermediate between Bitumen and
resin as has been shewn by Mr Hatchet

CharlesHatchett

He made his money from coach building which he used to pursue his interests in chemistry and mineralogy in the course of which he isolated the chemical element now called niobium. He was a Manager of the Royal Institution, playing a key role especially in running its lecture programme during the 1800s. FRSL.
