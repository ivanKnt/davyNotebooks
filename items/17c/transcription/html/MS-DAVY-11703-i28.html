Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Shale

Shale is a fine-grained, clastic sedimentary rock formed from mud that is a mix of flakes of clay minerals and tiny fragments of other minerals, especially quartz and calcite.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Calcite

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcite is a carbonate mineral and the most stable polymorph of calcium carbonate. Davy refers to it as Calcareous Spar.

✕

Pyrites

A generic term for sulphide minerals.

River Tees

The River Tees, in England, rises on the eastern slope of Cross Fell in the North Pennines and flows eastwards for 85 miles to reach the North Sea in the North East of England.
