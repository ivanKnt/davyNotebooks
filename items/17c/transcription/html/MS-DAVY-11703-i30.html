Granite

A coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock.

✕

Quartz

Symbol/formula: SiO2

A hard, crystalline mineral composed of silica (silicon dioxide).

✕

Schorl

A form of tourmaline, a crystalline silicate mineral group in which boron is compounded with elements such as aluminium, iron, magnesium, sodium, lithium, or potassium.

✕

Iron

Symbol/formula: Fe

A strong, hard, magnetic silvery-grey metal. It is, by mass, the most common element on Earth, just ahead of oxygen, forming much of Earth's outer and inner core.

✕

Tin

Symbol/formula: Sn

A silvery-coloured metal, tin is soft enough to be cut with little force, and a bar of tin can be bent by hand with little effort.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Calcite

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcite is a carbonate mineral and the most stable polymorph of calcium carbonate. Davy refers to it as Calcareous Spar.
