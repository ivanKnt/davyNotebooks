Quartz

Symbol/formula: SiO2

A hard, crystalline mineral composed of silica (silicon dioxide).

✕

Micaceous shist

A rock composed of mica and quartz.

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Gold

Symbol/formula: Au

In pure form, gold is a bright, slightly orange-yellow, dense, soft, malleable, and ductile transition metal.

✕

Silver

Symbol/formula: Ag

A soft, white, lustrous transition metal, silver exhibits the highest electrical conductivity, thermal conductivity, and reflectivity of any metal.
