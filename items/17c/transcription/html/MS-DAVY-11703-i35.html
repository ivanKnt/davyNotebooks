Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.

✕

Fluorspar

Symbol/formula: CaF2

Fluorite (also called fluorspar) is the mineral form of calcium fluoride.

✕

Spar

Spar is an old mining or mineralogy term used to refer to crystals that have readily discernible faces. Davy often specifies the type he is referring to, although this is evidently not the case here.

✕

Barytes

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

Now known as barium sulphate (BaSO4), a white crystalline solid, occurring as the mineral barites.

✕

Quartz

Symbol/formula: SiO2

A hard, crystalline mineral composed of silica (silicon dioxide).

✕

Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

In modern nomenclature, a mineral consisting of barium sulphate. In Davy's terminology it might have been equivalent to baryta, BaO.

✕

Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.

✕

Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Shale

Shale is a fine-grained, clastic sedimentary rock formed from mud that is a mix of flakes of clay minerals and tiny fragments of other minerals, especially quartz and calcite.
