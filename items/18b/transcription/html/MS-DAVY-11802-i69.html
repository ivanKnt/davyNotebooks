Mr Knight considers the lower part of the leaf
as the surface by which moisture is perspired
|_ but it appears from the expmts of Bonnel
that under certain circumstances this surface
is capable of absorbing moisture and the
power of acting upon elastic fluid is
likewise probably principally confined to
it -
When a plant is exposed to a factitious
atmosphere containing a certain proportion
of Carbonic Acid , being under proper circum
circumstances with regard to Heat and
light it is found after a certain time
that a part of the Carbonic Acid is
absorbed; but the Oxygene gas is not
diminished.

Elastic fluid

A descriptive term for a gas, emphasising its elastic quality of spontaneous expansion.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.
