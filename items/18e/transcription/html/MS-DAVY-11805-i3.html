The fertility of a soil depends upon the composition
of its texture but however perfect it may be in these
respects it requires great skill and great knowledge
of cultivation to preserve lands in that state in which
they are capable of producing the greatest quantity
of useful vegetable food -
The processes of farming by which the Earth is
rendered permanently productive are for the most
part mechanical but their effects are in some measure
chemical; for in all cases ther are connected with
modification and changes in the constitution of
the soil
