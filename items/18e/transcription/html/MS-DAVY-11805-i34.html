Vege Inert vegetable matter when existing
in a soil |in considerable quantity| seems to be prejudicial
the dead fibres stop the process
of the living roots & when dry
prevent them from being kept
in a due state of moisture -
By burning inert vegetable matter
made has its texture destroyed
is destroyed - - & this alone in
many cases must be beneficial.
| but the soil is not merely
freed from a substance prejudicial
to the functions of plants; but
this substance is converted into
a manure. -
