History of Natural Science

The primary faculties and feelings
of the human mind are the same in all
ages and in all climates -
And the experience obtained during the
progress of society constitutes the great
foundation of every species of knowledge.
A high and a just importance in
consequence has been attached by all Enlightened
minds to Historical details. -
They have always been regarded with
deep interest as affording rules of action and
instances of Conduct as exhibiting representations

This lecture was noted in the Royal Institution's minutes from their managers' meetings for the 14th January 1805 [see Archives of the Royal Institution, Minutes of the Managers’ Meetings, 1799-1903, 15 volumes in 7 (Scholar Press, London, 1971-1976), Vol 4, p. 9]. Although intended to take place on Thursday 9th May 1805 at 2pm, the lecture was probably not delivered. The notebook is written in the hand of an amanuensis.
