But it ought never to be forgotten that it was
from that happy country that the sources of our
own Science were either immediately or remotely
derived.
In Attica indeed the springs of Knowledge
were scanty and their course limited; - for
For |us| they flow in depth in fullness and
in strength: - But it is important to trace
the progress of Philosophy even from its most
slender beginnings, to view the mind in the
infancy of its improvement opposing its strength
to the imperfection of it methods, producing
truth by destroying error.
And Scientific History offers instances no less
useful than interesting to the active philosophical
Enquirer instances applicable to his pursuits
and guides to his exertions. -

Attica

or the Attic Peninsula, is a historical region that encompasses the entire modern Athens metropolitan area. In ancient times, Attica corresponded with the Athens city-state and was the most prominent region in Ancient Greece.
