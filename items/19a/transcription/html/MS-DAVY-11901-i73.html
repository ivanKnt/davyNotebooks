In the age of Thales Philosophy was respected
and honoured: He was considered as a benefactor
to his Country - and lived in the fulness of
Glory to a good old age
He died about 548 years before Christ at
the Olympic Games - And his Countrymen
honoured him with a monument and with an
inscription composed in the usual stile of
simplicity of the Greek Epigram -
Here Thales rest the wise
Small his tomb
But great his fame
Attested by the skies. -
Thales had many disciples even amongst
the Rich and great men of his own Nation
but none of them are recorded in History as
having modified or extended his doctrine except
Anixamander -

Anaximander

A philosopher who belonged to the Milesian school and learned the teachings of his master Thales. He succeeded Thales and became the second master of that school where he counted Anaximenes and, arguably, Pythagoras amongst his pupils.
