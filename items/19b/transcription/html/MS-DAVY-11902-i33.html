Aristotle

A pupil of Plato's whose philosophical idealism he came to reject, arguing instead for the primacy of observations of natural phenomena. He thus established the divide between theory and experiment in science that can still be detected today. Aristotle's writings became the main way the natural world was understood from the Middle Ages to the seventeenth century, especially in the universities.

SilenusCalatinus

Sicilian Greek historian of Magna Graecia who wrote a history in Greek of Hannibal's campaign in Italy from 218 to 204 BC. His work is known only from fragments and borrowings by other authors.

Lysander

A Spartan military and political leader. He destroyed the Athenian fleet at the Battle of Aegospotami in 405 BCE, forcing Athens to capitulate and bringing the Peloponnesian War to an end.

Thrace

Thrace is a geographical and historical region in Southeast Europe. Bounded by the Balkan Mountains to the north, the Aegean Sea to the south, and the Black Sea to the east, it comprises present-day southeastern Bulgaria, northeastern Greece, and the European part of Turkey, roughly the Roman Province of Thrace.

Gos

Aegospotami is the ancient Greek name for a small river issuing into the Hellespont northeast of Sestos. According to Pliny and Aristotle, a meteor landed here in 467 BC after reports of a comet.
