Pythagoras

Pythagoras of Samos was an ancient Ionian Greek philosopher, polymath, and the eponymous founder of Pythagoreanism. Believed in the primacy of number.

✕

Hermippus

Hermippus of Smyrna. A Greek grammarian, philosopher and biographer. Author of Lives - an extensively quoted but now lost classical work.

✕

Iamblichus

an Arab neoplatonic philosopher. He was the biographer of the Greek mystic, philosopher, and mathematician Pythagoras.

✕

Crotone

a port city in Calabria, southern Italy. Founded c. 710 BC as the Achaean colony of Kroton it became a great Greek city, home of the renowned mathematician-philosopher Pythagoras.

Samos

A Greek island in the eastern Aegean Sea, separated from Turkey by the mile-wide Mycale Strait. It was the birthplace of mathematician Pythagoras and philosopher Epicurus.
