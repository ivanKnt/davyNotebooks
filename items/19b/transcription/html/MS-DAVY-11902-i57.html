Timeus the Locrian

a character in two of Plato's dialogues, Timaeus and Critias. In both, he appears as a philosopher of the Pythagorean school. If there ever existed a historical Timaeus of Locri, he would have lived in the fifth century BCE, but his historicity is dubious since he only appears as a literary figure in Plato's works.

Epicharmus

a Greek dramatist and philosopher who is often credited with being one of the first comic writers.

Archytas

Ancient Greek mathematician, music theorist, statesman and strategist. He was a scientist and philosopher affiliated with the Pythagorean school and famous for being the reputed founder of mathematical mechanics.

✕

Empedocles

Ancient Greek philosopher whose philosophy is best known for originating the cosmogonic theory of the four classical elements.
