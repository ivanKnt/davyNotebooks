Archimedes

Ancient Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer, astronomer and inventor from Syracuse in Sicily.

✕

Euclid

An ancient Greek mathematician, geometer and logician. Considered the "father of geometry", he is chiefly known for the Elements treatise, which established the foundations of geometry that largely dominated the field until the early 19th century.

✕

Alexandria

The second largest city in Egypt and the largest city on the Mediterranean coast. Alexandria was the intellectual and cultural centre of the ancient Mediterranean for much of the Hellenistic age and late antiquity, particularly famous for its lighthouse and library.

Pharos

The Lighthouse of Alexandria, or the Pharos Lighthouse, was built on an island offshore from the Nile Delta, between 280-247 BCE. It was estimated to be 100m high. It was damaged by earthquakes and abandoned as a ruin in 1323 CE.

Ptolemaic dynasty

The Ptolemaic dynasty a Macedonian Greek royal house which ruled the Ptolemaic Kingdom in Ancient Egypt during the Hellenistic period. Reigning for 275 years, the Ptolemaic was the longest and last dynasty of ancient Egypt
