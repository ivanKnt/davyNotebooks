Alexandria

The second largest city in Egypt and the largest city on the Mediterranean coast. Alexandria was the intellectual and cultural centre of the ancient Mediterranean for much of the Hellenistic age and late antiquity, particularly famous for its lighthouse and library.

Aristarchus

an ancient Greek astronomer and mathematician who presented the first known heliocentric model that placed the Sun at the center of the known universe.

✕

Eratosthenes

a Greek polymath: a mathematician, geographer, poet, astronomer, and music theorist. He was the chief librarian at the Library of Alexandria.
