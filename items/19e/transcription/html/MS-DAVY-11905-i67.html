The elegant models of the Etruscan
vases produced by the ingenuity of the
late excellent Mr Wedgwood may be said
to have no immediate application to
common uses; but yet in consequence of
their invention a spirit of immitation
and of emulation has operated upon
every branch of the porcelain manufacture
and even the form and composition
of our common pitchers and our common
flower pots in consequence have been
improved -.
In certain departments of industry
still greater advantages result from
the constant attempts to attain the
highest degree of excellence.-
