In every perfect vegetable there are found
at least four distinct classes of organs
which though very different as to their
size and appearances are in some respects
similar in construction.
The roots the trunk and branches
The leaves and the flowers or seeds -
The roots the trunk and branches are
chiefly alike as to original structure and
they are composed of analogous parts
When the trunk of a tree is cut
transversely there is perceived at least
three different bodies the bark the wood
and the pith and these bodies are are
again capable of a new division
