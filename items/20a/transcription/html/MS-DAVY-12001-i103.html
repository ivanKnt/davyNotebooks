Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Ammonium sulphate

Symbol/formula: (NH4)2SO4

Sulphate of ammonia.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Ammonium nitrate

Symbol/formula: NH4NO3

A white crystalline salt consisting of ions of ammonium and nitrate; previously called phosnitrate of ammoniac by Davy.

✕

Iron oxide

Symbol/formula: Fe2O3

Oxide of iron. A dark red solid which occurs as the mineral haematite and as the main component of rust.

✕

Sulphite

Symbol/formula: SO

Any salt or ester of sulphurous acid, containing the ions SO32- or HSO3- (hydrogen sulphite) or the groups -SO3 or -HSO3. The salts are usually soluble crystalline compounds.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

French analytical chemist. Author of the 1794 paper 'Researches on Prussian Blue', in which he first set down the law of definite proportions, or Proust's law, as it is commonly known.
