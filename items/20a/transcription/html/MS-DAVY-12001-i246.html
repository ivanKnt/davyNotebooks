Medical Pneumatic Institution

By 1798, located in 6-7 Dowry Square, Hotwells, Clifton, Bristol. Davy was employed by Thomas Beddoes to work as its Superindendent.

View location on Google Maps
✕

WilliamNicholson

Founding editor of A Journal of Natural Philosophy, Chemistry, and the Arts (1797-1814) where Davy published some of his early papers. Part of the Godwin circle.

✕

Gaseous oxide of azote

Symbol/formula: N2O

Nitrous oxide. Davy initially refered to nitrous oxide as 'gaseous oxide of azote', but changed to 'nitrous oxide' on the discovery that the gas was respirable.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.
