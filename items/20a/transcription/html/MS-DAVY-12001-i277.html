quere

query; to enquire (Latin)

✕

LuigiGalvani

Italian surgeon and anatomist who carried out experiments with electricity and animals. The term 'galvanism' was named after him

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Phosoxygen

Davy's working name for the combination of light and oxygen. He published his theories in a collection of essays entitled On Heat, Light, and the Combinations of Light, On Phos-oxygen and its Combinations (1799).

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Sulphuric acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen. It is a colourless, odourless, and viscous liquid that is miscible with water.

✕

Phosphorus

Symbol/formula: P

A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.

✕

Carbon

Symbol/formula: C

A non-metallic and tetravalent element. Well-known allotropes include graphite, diamond, amorphous carbon, and fullerenes.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.

✕

Boron

Symbol/formula: B

A non-metallic solid substance, believed to be a metal before it was isolated by Davy.

✕

Antimony

Symbol/formula: Sb

A lustrous gray metalloid, it is found in nature mainly as the sulphide mineral stibnite. Antimony compounds have been known since ancient times and were powdered for use as medicine and cosmetics, often known by the Arabic name 'kohl'.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Muriatic acid

Symbol/formula: HCl

Hydrochloric acid. Davy also refers to this as muriatic gas and marine acid.

✕

Mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg

Also known as quicksilver. A heavy, silvery element, mercury is the only metallic element that is known to be liquid at standard temperature and pressure.

✕

Platina

An alloy of platinum and several other metals, including palladium, osmium, and iridium.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.
