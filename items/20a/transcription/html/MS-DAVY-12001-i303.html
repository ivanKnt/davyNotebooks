Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Phosoxygen

Davy's working name for the combination of light and oxygen. He published his theories in a collection of essays entitled On Heat, Light, and the Combinations of Light, On Phos-oxygen and its Combinations (1799).

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Phosacids

Davy used the phos to refer to these acids as chemical compounds that contained light as an element.

✕

LuigiGalvani

Italian surgeon and anatomist who carried out experiments with electricity and animals. The term 'galvanism' was named after him
