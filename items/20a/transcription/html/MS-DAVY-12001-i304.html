Animal functions, theory of respiration
Laws of Life. -
[Four horizontal rules, of diminishing size]
Hartleys Theory is inadequate to account
for the phænomena, Memory is the
power of associating ideas together in
the order in which their causative
sensations were received. Science depends
on our connecting together a number of
ideas with some ideas or terms capable
of recalling them all ie on our
power of generalising our ideas -
Generalisation is the associating of ideas
in succession from their common properties
& since they agree giving to all of
them a common name - This name
or general term calls up a particular
idea that another & so on -
