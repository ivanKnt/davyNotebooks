Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Gaseous oxide of azote

Symbol/formula: N2O

Nitrous oxide. Davy initially refered to nitrous oxide as 'gaseous oxide of azote', but changed to 'nitrous oxide' on the discovery that the gas was respirable.

✕

I stated to you in April

Davy wrote to William Nicholson on 17 April 1799 about his discovery that gaseous oxyd of azote (Nitrous oxide) is 'respirable' when freed from nitrous gas (nitric oxide). This may be a previously unidentified draft letter fragment addressed to Nicholson.
