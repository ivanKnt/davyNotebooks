Sulphate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaSO4

Calcium sulphate, gypsum.

✕

Strontian

Symbol/formula: Sr

Old terminology for strontium, an alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Barytes

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

Now known as barium sulphate (BaSO4), a white crystalline solid, occurring as the mineral barites.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Phosphate of lime

Symbol/formula: Ca3(PO4)2

Tricalcium phosphate (sometimes abbreviated TCP) is a calcium salt of phosphoric acid.

✕

Strontium sulphate

Symbol/formula: SrSO4

The sulfate salt of strontium.

✕

Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

In modern nomenclature, a mineral consisting of barium sulphate. In Davy's terminology it might have been equivalent to baryta, BaO.

✕

Carbonate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcium carbonate.

✕

Carbonate of magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgCO3

Magnesium carbonate.

✕

Phosphorus

Symbol/formula: P

A non-metallic chemical element of the nitrogen family. A colourless, semitransparent, soft, waxy solid.

✕

Fluate of lime

A mineral formed by the combination of fluoric acid with lime.
