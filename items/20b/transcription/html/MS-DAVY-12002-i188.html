Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Strontian

Symbol/formula: Sr

Old terminology for strontium, an alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Flint

Occasionally flintstone. A sedimentary cryptocrystalline form of the mineral quartz, categorised as the variety of chert that occurs in chalk or marly limestone.

✕

Carbonate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcium carbonate.

✕

Fluate of lime

A mineral formed by the combination of fluoric acid with lime.

✕

Phosphate of lime

Symbol/formula: Ca3(PO4)2

Tricalcium phosphate (sometimes abbreviated TCP) is a calcium salt of phosphoric acid.

✕

Copper

Symbol/formula: Cu

A soft, malleable, and ductile metal with very high thermal and electrical conductivity. A freshly exposed surface of pure copper has a pinkish-orange colour.

✕

Pyrites

A generic term for sulphide minerals.

✕

Gypsum

Symbol/formula: CaSO4·2H2O

A soft white or grey mineral consisting of hydrated calcium sulphate.

✕

Steel

Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon with improved strength and fracture resistance compared to other forms of iron.

✕

Bismuth

Symbol/formula: Bi

A high-density, silvery, pink-tinged metal.

✕

Antimony

Symbol/formula: Sb

A lustrous gray metalloid, it is found in nature mainly as the sulphide mineral stibnite. Antimony compounds have been known since ancient times and were powdered for use as medicine and cosmetics, often known by the Arabic name 'kohl'.

✕

Zinc

Symbol/formula: Zn

A a slightly brittle metal at room temperature and has a shiny-greyish appearance when oxidation is removed.
