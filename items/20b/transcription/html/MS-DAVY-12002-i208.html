Fluoric acid

Symbol/formula: HF

Hydrofluoric acid. A solution of hydrogen fluoride in water.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Fluate

A now obsolete term for a fluoride.

✕

Ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH3

An inorganic chemical compound of nitrogen and hydrogen. Ammonia is a colourless gas with a distinct pungent smell.

✕

Muriate of ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH4Cl

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline salt that is highly soluble in water. Solutions of ammonium chloride are mildly acidic. In its naturally occurring mineralogic form, it is known as sal ammoniac.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Ammonium sulphate

Symbol/formula: (NH4)2SO4

Sulphate of ammonia.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Sulfurous acid

Symbol/formula: H2SO3

Sulphuric acid. A mineral acid composed of the elements sulphur, oxygen, and hydrogen.

✕

a smiling man

Person, untraced, inhaling nitrous oxide.

✕

retort

In a chemistry laboratory, a retort is a device used for distillation or dry distillation of substances.
