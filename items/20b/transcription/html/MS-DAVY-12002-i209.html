Phosoxygen

Davy's working name for the combination of light and oxygen. He published his theories in a collection of essays entitled On Heat, Light, and the Combinations of Light, On Phos-oxygen and its Combinations (1799).

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Phosphine

Symbol/formula: PH3

Hydrogen phosphide. Pure phosphine is odourless, but technical grade samples have a highly unpleasant odour like rotting fish.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Muriate of ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH4Cl

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline salt that is highly soluble in water. Solutions of ammonium chloride are mildly acidic. In its naturally occurring mineralogic form, it is known as sal ammoniac.
