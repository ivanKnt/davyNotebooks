July 13. The blood which was exposed yesterday
is of a dark crimson - & an absorbtion
of 1 & 1/3 has taken place, the remaining
gas will not support the flame of a
taper as well as before -
both the plants took very well & in
both a slight absorbtion has taken place
this morning a porcelain tube was heated
red & muriate of ammonia inserted into
that end which was cool, gas slowly
came over & about 12 cubic inches
were collected which appeared to
be a mixture of hydrogen , oxygen & nitrogen
Miss. T. breathed this morning six
quarts of gaseous oxyd , then began to breath
without any apprehension & breathed remarkably
well took full inspirations, in about a
minute she suffered the bag to drop
She then began to sob most violently
then cryed & laughed alternately she
used most vivid muscular actions &
appeared to be perfectly delerious in about
ten minutes this went of altogether

Muriate of ammonia

Symbol/formula: NH4Cl

An inorganic compound and a white crystalline salt that is highly soluble in water. Solutions of ammonium chloride are mildly acidic. In its naturally occurring mineralogic form, it is known as sal ammoniac.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

Miss. T.

Person, untraced. Miss T's experiences after inhaling nitrous oxide are not included in Davy's published version Researches, Chemical, and Philosophical.
