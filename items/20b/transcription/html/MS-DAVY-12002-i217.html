Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Phosphine

Symbol/formula: PH3

Hydrogen phosphide. Pure phosphine is odourless, but technical grade samples have a highly unpleasant odour like rotting fish.

✕

Calcium hydroxide

Symbol/formula: Ca(OH)2

Produced when quicklime is mixed with water. Traditionally called slacked lime.

✕

Phosphoric acid

Symbol/formula: H3PO4

A corrosive acid that can form three different classes of salts, namely primary phosphates, dibasic phosphates, and tribasic phosphates. It is corrosive to ferrous metals and alloys.

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Green vitriol

Symbol/formula: FeSO4

Iron(II) sulphate, also known as ferrous sulphate.
