LuigiGalvani

Italian surgeon and anatomist who carried out experiments with electricity and animals. The term 'galvanism' was named after him to refer to the generation of electric current by chemical action.

✕

PatrickDwyer

Assisted Davy during his research into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide at the Medical Pneumatic Intitution.

✕

JohnKing

Nicholas Johann Koenig, Bristol-based surgeon, painter and linguist, originally from Bern, Switzerland. A friend of Davy's in Bristol, King took his position at the Medical Pneumatic Institute after Davy left for London in 1801. King married Emmeline Edgeworth in 1803.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Guinea pigs

Davy experimented on guinea pigs when trialling nitrous oxide; see Researches, Chemical and Philosophical; Chiefly Concerning Nitrous Oxide: Or Dephlogisticated Nitrous Air, and Its Respiration (London, 1800)
