Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Nitrous oxide

Symbol/formula: N2O

Later known as 'laughing gas'. Davy discovered that nitrous oxide was respirable when not contaminated with nitric oxide (NO). Davy researched into the physiological effects of nitrous oxide.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Nitric acid

Symbol/formula: HNO3

A highly corrosive mineral acid. The compound is colourless, but samples tend to acquire a yellow cast over time due to decomposition into oxides of nitrogen

✕

Strontian

Symbol/formula: Sr

Old terminology for strontium, an alkaline earth metal, a soft silver-white yellowish metallic element that is highly chemically reactive. The metal forms a dark oxide layer when it is exposed to air.

✕

Baryte

Symbol/formula: BaSO4

In modern nomenclature, a mineral consisting of barium sulphate. In Davy's terminology it might have been equivalent to baryta, BaO.

✕

Nitrous acid

Symbol/formula: HNO2

A weak and monoprotic acid known only in solution, in the gas phase and in the form of nitrite salts.

✕

HenryCavendish

A central figure in English natural philosophy and chemistry, among other research he determined the density of the earth and discovered what would later be named hydrogen. He was one of the people who helped appoint Davy to the Royal Institution. Following his death in 1810 Davy delivered a widely-quoted eulogy on Cavendish and later arranged for some of his apparatus to be transferred to the Royal Institution. FRSL.
