LuigiGalvani

Italian surgeon and anatomist who carried out experiments with electricity and animals. The term 'galvanism' was named after him to refer to the generation of electric current by chemical action.

✕

AlessandroVolta

Professor of experimental physics at the University of Pavia. At the end of the eighteenth century he Invented the Voltaic pile which produced electric current and which Davy shortly dubbed the battery. Davy described meeting him in 1814 in notebook 14/I, p.38. ForMemRSL.

✕

JohnKing

Nicholas Johann Koenig, Bristol-based surgeon, painter and linguist, originally from Bern, Switzerland. A friend of Davy's in Bristol, King took his position at the Medical Pneumatic Institute after Davy left for London in 1801. King married Emmeline Edgeworth in 1803.

✕

Nitrogene

Symbol/formula: N

Now known as nitrogen. A colourless and odourless gas, nitrogen is non-combustible and non-toxic. Davy also refers to this as azote.

✕

Hydrogene

Symbol/formula: H

Now known as hydrogen. Hydrogen is the lightest element and, at standard conditions, is a gas of diatomic molecules. It is colourless, odourless, tasteless, non-toxic, and highly combustible.

✕

Common air

The air of the lower stratum of the atmosphere, or the air that we breathe, which is a mixture of several gases, including oxygen (O2), nitrogen (N2) and carbon dioxide (CO2).

✕

Hydrocarbonate

Symbol/formula: H2 + CO

Hydrogen mixed with carbon monoxide.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).
