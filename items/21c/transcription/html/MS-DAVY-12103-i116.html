Alburnum

sapwood; the soft, newer wood in the trunk of a tree found between the bark and the hardened heartwood.

✕

Louis NicolasVauquelin

French pharmacist and chemist who met Davy in Paris in 1813. ForMemRSL.

✕

JohnMayow

Cornish physiologist and chemist, investigated respiration especially. FRSL.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Saline

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Sodium chloride in solution.

✕

Ammonium salts

Salts of ammonia. Most simple ammonium salts are very soluble in water.

✕

Carbonate of lime

Symbol/formula: CaCO3

Calcium carbonate.

✕

Nitre

Symbol/formula: KNO3

Potassium nitrate. The mineral form is saltpetre, which was useful as a fertiliser and for making gunpowder.

✕

Muriate of soda

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Also known as sodium chloride, or common salt.

✕

Nitrous acid

Symbol/formula: HNO2

A weak and monoprotic acid known only in solution, in the gas phase and in the form of nitrite salts.
