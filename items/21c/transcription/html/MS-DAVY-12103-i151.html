- The organs in the rising vegetable
that are most essential to its nourishment
are the vessels in the Alburnum or Sap
wood; which extend in all cases from the
extremity of the leaves to the minutest
fibres of the roots. -
Instance.
|_Numerous experiments demonstrate "that
the ascent of the fluids through these vessels
is immediately connected with vegetation
& with the life of the plant"
- The bark of a tree may be cut or
even stripped off from the trunk without
materially impeding the growth of the branches
above or below. -
- But when the Alburnum is cut
through so that the fluid in the vessels
can no longer pass beyond a certain height;
all the parts of the plant above the incision
