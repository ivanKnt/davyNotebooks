CharlesHatchett

He made his money from coach building which he used to pursue his interests in chemistry and mineralogy in the course of which he isolated the chemical element now called niobium. He was a Manager of the Royal Institution, playing a key role especially in running its lecture programme during the 1800s. FRSL.

✕

Aqua fortis

Archaic term for nitric acid.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Oxygene

Symbol/formula: O

Now known as oxygen. Oxygen is a colourless, odourless, tasteless gas essential to living organisms.

✕

Carbonic acid

Symbol/formula: CO2 or H2CO3

Now known as Carbon dioxide (CO2), or a solution of carbon dioxide in water (H2CO3).

✕

Mucilage

Mucilage is a thick gluey substance produced by nearly all plants and some microorganisms.
