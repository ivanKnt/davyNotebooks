Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Marl

Or marle. A loose, unconsolidated earthy deposit, consisting of clay mixed with calcium carbonate.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

JohnEvelyn

Writer, landowner, gardener, courtier, diarist, minor government official and a founding Fellow of the Royal Society of London. FRSL.

✕

SamuelHartlib

Born in Elbing (then in Royal (East) Prussia, now in Poland) of a Polish father and English mother, he moved to England in the late 1620s. Hartlib became a major intellectual figure and his 'circle' was one of the major pre-existing groups that helped form the Royal Society of London.

✕

EdwardLisle

Barrister of Moyles Court, Hampshire, who contributed significantly to Observations in Husbandry (1757) which his son published posthumously.

✕

JosephBlack

From 1766, Professor of Medcine and Chemistry at the University of Edinburgh. Discovered latent heat and fixed air, later renamed carbon dioxide. A correspondent of James Watt and Thomas Beddoes (who studied under him).
