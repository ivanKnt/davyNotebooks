Gelatine

A translucent, colourless, flavorless food ingredient, commonly derived from collagen taken from animal body parts.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Carbonate of potash

Symbol/formula: K2CO3

Potassium carbonate. It is a white salt, which is soluble in water and forms a strongly alkaline solution.

✕

Gallic acid

Symbol/formula: C7H6O5

A crystalline acid prepared from the oak-gall and other vegetable products, used as a tanning agent.

✕

Nitrate of mercury

Symbol/formula: Hg(NO3)2

Mercury(II) nitrate. Mercury nitrate salts, which are colourless or white and soluble, are occasionally used as a reagent. It is made by treating mercury with hot concentrated nitric acid.

Tannin

Biiter and astringent compounds found in plants. They include Ellagic acid, gallic acid, and pyrogallic acid which were first discovered by Braconnot in 1831.
