JamesHall

Scottish baronet (1776) and MP. Chemist and geologist who supported of James Hutton's theories of uniformitarian geology. FRSL.

✕

GregoryWatt

Youngest son of James Watt. Lodged with Davy's mother in Penzance during the winter of 1797-8 and became close friends with Davy.

✕

JamesWatt

A Scottish scientific instrument maker who studied with the chemist Joseph Black at Glasgow University. He developed the separate condenser for steam engines which increased their effieciency significantly. He took out a twenty-five-year patent (ending in 1800) and moved to Birmingham where he went into partnership with the businessman and industrialist Matthew Boulton. They made their fortune by having an effective monopoly of manufacturing steam engines. A friend of Thomas Beddoes, he met Davy on a number of occasions and Davy gave the oration at the unveiling of Watt's statue in Greenock. FRSL.
