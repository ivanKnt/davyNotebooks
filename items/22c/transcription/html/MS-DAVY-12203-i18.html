Borate of ammonia

An inorganic compound composed of boron, nitrogen, and hydrogen.

✕

Soda

An alkaline substance obtained from the ashes of marine or salt-impregnated plants, especially species of Salsola. Davy decomposed soda to reveal its elemental sodium.

✕

Silex

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Silica, an oxide of silicon.

✕

Alumine

Symbol/formula: AL2O3

Alumine is an archaic name for alumina, which is aluminium oxide.

✕

Potash

An alkaline substance consisting largely of impure potassium carbonate. In October 1807, Davy decomposed potash using electricity, revealing the previously unknown element potassium.

✕

Glucine

A monoclinic mineral containing beryllium, calcium, hydrogen, oxygen, and phosphorus.

✕

Lime

Symbol/formula: CaO

Commonly known as quicklime or burnt lime, calcium oxide is a widely used chemical compound. It is a white, caustic, alkaline, crystalline solid at room temperature.

✕

Magnesia

Symbol/formula: MgO

A white hygroscopic solid mineral that occurs naturally as periclase and is a source of magnesium.

✕

Manganese

Symbol/formula: Mn

A hard, brittle, silvery metal, often found in minerals in combination with iron. Manganese was first isolated in the 1770s.

✕

Borate of sodium

Any salt of sodium with an anion consisting of boron and oxygen, and possibly hydrogen, or any hydrate thereof.

✕

Muriate of soda

Symbol/formula: NaCl

Also known as sodium chloride, or common salt.

✕

Boric acid

Symbol/formula: B(OH)3

A compound of boron, oxygen, and hydrogen. Davy often refers to this as boracic acid.
