Shist

Or schist. Any metamorphic rock that can be split into thin layers because of its thin bands of micaceous minerals, with mineral grains.

✕

Sienite

Syenite. A coarse-grained intrusive igneous rock with a general composition similar to that of granite, but deficient in quartz, which, if present at all, occurs in relatively small concentrations.

✕

Serpentine

A metamorphic rock containing one or more of the serpentine group minerals.

✕

Porphyry

A hard, igneous purplish-red rock in which crystals of feldspar are embedded in a fine-grained groundmass.

✕

Micaceous shist

A rock composed of mica and quartz.

✕

Argillite

A fine-grained sedimentary rock.

✕

Silicious

Symbol/formula: SiO2

Siliceous rocks: any of a group of sedimentary rocks that consist largely or almost entirely of silicon dioxide.

Trass

A grey or cream-coloured fragmental rock.

✕

Limestone

A sedimentary rock composed mainly of calcium carbonate (CaCO3).

✕

Greywacke

Greywacke or graywacke is a type of sandstone.

✕

Breccia

A rock composed of large angular broken fragments of minerals or rocks cemented together by a fine-grained matrix.

✕

Shell limestone

A type of limestone composed largely of shells.

✕

Sandstone

A sedimentary rock consisting of sand or quartz grains cemented together, typically red, yellow, or brown in colour.

✕

Ironstone

A sedimentary rock, either deposited directly as a ferruginous sediment or created by chemical replacement, that contains a substantial proportion of an iron ore compound.

✕

Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.
