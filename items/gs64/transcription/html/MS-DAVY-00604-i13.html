JohnRay

A Christian English naturalist widely regarded as one of the earliest of the English parson-naturalists. Until 1670, he wrote his name as John Wray. FRS.

JohnWoodward

Natural philospher with an interest in geology. His collection of fossils and minerals is part of Cambridge University's Sedgwick Museum of Geology. FRSL.

RobertHooke

Natural philosopher who acted as experimental demonstrator at the Royal Society of London during its early years. Prominent in the study of air and microscopy amongst other subjects. FRSL.
