- Pit coal is uniformly |always| found
in secondary countries - Its basis
is the same substance as charcoal
but it is usually impregnated
with Bitumen ; which is the part
of the coal that fuses, that
produces |occasions| caking; & that emits
the vapour which produces flame.
- Coals differ very much according
as they contain different proportions
of fixed coaly matter & of Bitumen
And they are often adulterated
by Earths, & by the compounds of
Sulphur & iron, which I have
already mentioned under the name
of Pyrites . -
The best coal is that which
burns with a bright light

✕

Pitcoal

Coal mined from the earth - distinguished from charcoal.

✕

Charcoal

A lightweight black carbon residue produced by strongly heating wood (or other animal and plant materials) in minimal oxygen to remove all water and volatile constituents.

✕

Bitumen

A sticky, black, highly viscous liquid or semi-solid form of petroleum.

✕

Sulphur

Symbol/formula: S

Pure sulphur is a tasteless, odourless, brittle solid that is pale yellow in colour, a poor conductor of electricity, and insoluble in water.

✕

Pyrites

A generic term for sulphide minerals.
