Basalt

A dark grey to black dense to fine-grained igneous rock that consists of basic plagioclase, augite, and usually magnetite. More than 90% of volcanic rock on earth is basaltic.

✕

Breccia

A rock composed of large angular broken fragments of minerals or rocks cemented together by a fine-grained matrix.

✕

Tufa

A variety of limestone formed when carbonate minerals precipitate out of water at ambient temperature.

✕

Staffa

an island of the Inner Hebrides in Argyll and Bute, Scotland.
