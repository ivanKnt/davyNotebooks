Pliny

The Elder. Roman army officer and author who wrote an encyclopedic Natural History. Died in the aftermath of the eruption of Vesuvius.

✕

Reference

Pliny the Elder (77)Natural History.

✕

Cornu Ammonis

Meaning, the horns of Ammon, or Ammon's horns; curling ram's horns used as a symbol of the Egyptian god Ammon. Also used to describe the visually similar fossil shells of ancient snails and cephalods, which led to the latter becoming known as ammonite.

✕

Glossopetra

Or, tongue-stone. A rock resembing a human tongue and once thought to have medicinal properties. Now known to be a fossilised shark's tooth.
